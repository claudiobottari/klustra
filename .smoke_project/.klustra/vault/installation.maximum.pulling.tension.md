## Overview

**Installation maximum pulling tension** is the highest tensile force that may be applied to a [[high-voltage.cable.systems|high-voltage cable]] during pulling operations, typically expressed in newtons per square millimetre (N/mm²) of conductor cross‑section. Exceeding this limit can cause permanent elongation, necking, or breakage of the [[copper.conductor|copper]] or [[aluminium.conductor|aluminium conductor]], and may damage the [[xlpe.insulation|XLPE insulation]] or [[metallic.sheath|metallic sheath]].

## Limit value

During installation, the maximum pulling tension must not exceed **50 N/mm²** on the conductor.^[8913d160a82c281a:2] This value applies regardless of conductor material (copper or aluminium) and is a general requirement for safe pulling.

## Related installation parameters

- [[installation.minimum.bending.radius|Minimum bending radius]] – typically 20× cable outer diameter during installation.^[8913d160a82c281a:2]
- [[installation.temperature|Installation temperature]] – must be above 0 °C.^[8913d160a82c281a:2]
- Pulling speed – a steady rate of 5–10 m/min is recommended.^[8913d160a82c281a:4]
- [[installation.cable.lubricant|Cable lubricant]] – must be compatible with the outer sheath material.^[8913d160a82c281a:4]
- [[installation.cable.winch|Cable winch]] – must be equipped with tension monitoring.^[8913d160a82c281a:4]

## Practical considerations

- Ducts must be cleaned and inspected before pulling.^[8913d160a82c281a:4]
- For [[installation.direct.burial|direct burial]], trench depth, sand bedding, and warning tape requirements are specified separately.^[8913d160a82c281a:5]
- Jointing (see [[installation.jointing]]) should be performed in a controlled environment; [[installation.prefabricated.joints|prefabricated joints]] are preferred for speed.^[8913d160a82c281a:6]

## Storia e revisioni

No conflicting claims were found in the provided sources. The 50 N/mm² limit is the only stated value.
