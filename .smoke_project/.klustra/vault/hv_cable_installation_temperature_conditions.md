## Temperature Conditions for HV Cable Installation

Installation of [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] must be carried out when the ambient temperature is **above 0 °C**. This requirement applies to all installation methods, including duct, direct burial, and jointing operations.^[8913d160a82c281a:1]

### Rationale
- **Cable flexibility**: At temperatures below 0 °C, the outer sheath and [[xlpe_insulation]] become brittle, increasing the risk of cracking or damage during bending and pulling.^[8913d160a82c281a:1]
- **Lubricant performance**: Cable lubricants may thicken or solidify below freezing, reducing their effectiveness and potentially causing sheath abrasion.^[8913d160a82c281a:1]
- **Jointing integrity**: [[hv_cable_jointing_requirements]] specify a controlled environment; low ambient temperatures can compromise the curing or sealing of joint components.^[8913d160a82c281a:1]

### Related Installation Parameters
- [[hv_cable_installation_bending_radius]]: Minimum 20× cable outer diameter during installation.^[8913d160a82c281a:1]
- [[hv_cable_installation_pulling_tension]]: Maximum 50 N/mm² on the conductor.^[8913d160a82c82a:1]

### Compliance
Installation temperature conditions are part of the [[hv_cable_regulatory_framework]] and must be verified on site before commencing any cable pulling or jointing activity.^[8913d160a82c281a:1]
