## Overview

Water treeing is a primary degradation mechanism in [[xlpe.insulation]] used in high-voltage cable systems. It is one of the two main aging processes, alongside [[electrical.treeing]], that limit the service life of XLPE-insulated cables.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29]

## Mechanism

The exact mechanism of water treeing involves the growth of microscopic, tree-like structures within the insulation under the combined influence of an electric field and moisture. These structures initiate from imperfections or contaminants in the insulation and propagate over time, eventually reducing the dielectric strength of the material.

## Mitigation

Modern manufacturing techniques, such as the use of [[super.clean.xlpe.compound]]s and [[antioxidant.package]]s, significantly suppress water treeing. These advanced materials enable design lifetimes exceeding 40 years for XLPE-insulated cables.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29]
