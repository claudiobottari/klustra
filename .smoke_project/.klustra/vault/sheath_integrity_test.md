## Overview

A **sheath integrity test** is an after-installation commissioning test performed on [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] to verify the continuity and insulation condition of the metallic sheath or screen. The test is typically conducted at 10 kV DC for 1 minute.^[362871cd3841da0e:testing_procedures.md]

## Procedure

The sheath integrity test is part of the on-site commissioning tests for high-voltage cable installations. It is performed after the cable has been installed and jointed, but before the system is put into service. The test applies a direct current (DC) voltage of 10 kV between the metallic sheath and ground for a duration of 1 minute.^[362871cd3841da0e:testing_procedures.md]

## Purpose

The test detects defects in the outer sheath, such as punctures, cuts, or moisture ingress, which could compromise the long-term reliability of the cable system. A failure during the test indicates a loss of sheath integrity that must be repaired before commissioning.^[362871cd3841da0e:testing_procedures.md]

## Related tests

The sheath integrity test is one of several after-installation tests specified in standards such as [[IEC 62067]]. Other commissioning tests include:
- [[high_voltage_withstand_test]] (DC or VLF withstand testing)
- [[partial_discharge_measurement]]
- [[joint_resistance_measurement]]^[362871cd3841da0e:testing_procedures.md]
