## Overview

The **sheath integrity test** is an after-installation commissioning test performed on high-voltage cable systems to verify the continuity and insulation condition of the [[metallic.sheath]] and its outer protective jacket. It is typically conducted at 10 kV DC for 1 minute.^[362871cd3841da0e:testing_procedures.md]

## Purpose

The test ensures that the metallic sheath—which serves as a return path for fault currents and as a shield against electromagnetic interference—has not been damaged during installation (e.g., by excessive [[bending.radii]] or [[installation.maximum.pulling.tension]]) and that the outer [[outer.protective.jacket]] is free of punctures or abrasions that could allow moisture ingress. A failed sheath integrity test may indicate a breach that could lead to [[water.treeing]] or [[electrical.treeing]] in the [[xlpe.insulation]].

## Procedure

1. **Isolation and earthing**: The cable is isolated and earthed in accordance with [[cable.isolation.and.earthing]] and [[local.electrical.safety.regulations]].
2. **Voltage application**: A DC voltage of 10 kV is applied between the metallic sheath and earth for 1 minute.^[362871cd3841da0e:testing_procedures.md]
3. **Measurement**: The leakage current is monitored; a sudden increase or breakdown indicates a fault.
4. **Evaluation**: The test is passed if no breakdown occurs and the leakage current remains within acceptable limits (typically < 1 mA, depending on cable length and manufacturer specifications).

## Standards

The test is part of the after-installation testing suite defined in [[iec.62067]] and is complementary to other commissioning tests such as [[cable.testing.dc_vlf_withstand_testing]] and [[cable.testing.joint_resistance_measurement]].^[362871cd3841da0e:testing_procedures.md]

## Safety considerations

- The test must be performed within a [[safe.work.zone.with.barriers]] and with [[exclusion.zone.for.cable.fault]] in place.
- [[fault.re-energization.prohibition]] applies until the test is completed and the cable is declared safe.
- All personnel must follow [[company.specific.safety.procedures]] and report to the [[control.centre.reporting]] before and after the test.
