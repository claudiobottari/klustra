## Overview

The **semi-conductive screen** is a critical component of high-voltage (HV) cable systems, positioned directly over the conductor and under the [[xlpe.insulation]] layer. Its primary function is to create a smooth, equipotential surface that eliminates air gaps and prevents partial discharges at the conductor–insulation interface.^[8085135b0a96974f:section:High-Voltage Cable Systems]

## Construction and materials

The screen is applied as a thin, extruded layer of a carbon-black‑filled polymer compound. The material is formulated to have a controlled resistivity (typically in the range of 10²–10⁴ Ω·cm) so that it is sufficiently conductive to equalise the electric field but not so conductive as to create a short circuit. The compound is co‑extruded with the insulation in a [[triple.extrusion.process]], ensuring intimate contact and freedom from voids.^[8085135b0a96974f:section:High-Voltage Cable Systems]

## Function in the cable system

- **Field grading:** By providing a smooth, cylindrical equipotential surface, the screen eliminates local field enhancements that could initiate [[electrical.treeing]].^[8085135b0a96974f:section:High-Voltage Cable Systems]
- **Partial‑discharge suppression:** Any roughness or protrusion on the conductor would create high‑stress points; the semi‑conductive screen encapsulates these irregularities and prevents micro‑discharges.^[8085135b0a96974f:section:High-Voltage Cable Systems]
- **Thermal and mechanical stability:** The screen material is chosen to withstand the curing temperature of the XLPE during [[peroxide.crosslinking]] and to remain flexible during cable bending.^[8085135b0a96974f:section:High-Voltage Cable Systems]

## Relationship to other components

The semi‑conductive screen is the innermost of three co‑extruded layers: conductor screen → [[xlpe.insulation]] → insulation screen. Together they form the core of a modern HV cable, which is then enclosed by a [[metallic.sheath]] (for water‑blocking) and an [[outer.protective.jacket]].^[8085135b0a96974f:section:High-Voltage Cable Systems]
