## Overview

Water treeing is a primary aging mechanism in cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) insulation for high-voltage cable systems operating at 66 kV and above.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material] It involves the formation of microscopic, tree-like structures within the insulation material, driven by the presence of moisture, electric stress, and impurities. These structures grow over time, reducing the dielectric strength of the insulation and potentially leading to electrical failure.

## Mechanism

Water treeing occurs when moisture penetrates the XLPE insulation, typically through defects or at interfaces with semi-conductive screens. Under the influence of the electric field, water molecules and ions form dendritic (tree-like) paths. The process is accelerated by the presence of contaminants, voids, or mechanical stress. Unlike electrical treeing, water treeing does not involve partial discharge but still degrades the insulation's dielectric properties.

## Impact on Cable Performance

Water treeing reduces the dielectric strength of XLPE insulation, which is normally 20–30 kV/mm.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material] It can lead to a gradual increase in dielectric loss (tan δ) and a decrease in the insulation's ability to withstand operating voltage. Over time, water trees can transition into electrical trees, causing catastrophic failure. The design lifetime of XLPE cables, which can exceed 40 years with modern super-clean compounds, is significantly shortened by water treeing.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]

## Mitigation

Modern super-clean XLPE compounds with antioxidant packages are designed to minimize impurities that nucleate water trees.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material] Manufacturing processes such as the triple-extrusion process, which applies conductor screen, insulation, and insulation screen in a single pass, eliminate interfaces that could trap moisture.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material] Additionally, proper cable design, including metallic sheaths and outer protective jackets, prevents moisture ingress. Testing methods like partial discharge measurement and DC VLF withstand testing can detect water treeing before failure.

## Relation to Other Degradation Mechanisms

Water treeing is distinct from [[electrical_treeing]], which involves partial discharge and rapid growth. However, water treeing can precede and initiate electrical treeing, making it a critical precursor to failure. Both are primary aging mechanisms in XLPE insulation.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]
