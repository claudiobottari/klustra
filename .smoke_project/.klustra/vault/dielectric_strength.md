## Dielectric Strength of XLPE Insulation

**Dielectric strength** is the maximum electric field that a material can withstand without breaking down. For cross-linked polyethylene ([[xlpe_insulation_material]]) used in [[high_voltage_cable_systems]], the dielectric strength is typically in the range of **20–30 kV/mm** at room temperature.^[57e907ec63549ebf:10]

This property is a key factor in the insulation design of cables operating at 66 kV and above, where the insulation thickness is chosen to keep the electric stress well below the breakdown threshold. The dielectric strength of XLPE is influenced by the manufacturing process (e.g., [[peroxide_crosslinking]] and [[triple_extrusion_process]]) and by the presence of contaminants or defects that can initiate [[water_treeing]] or [[electrical_treeing]].^[57e907ec63549ebf:10]

Modern super-clean XLPE compounds achieve long-term dielectric strength stability, supporting a [[design_lifetime]] exceeding 40 years under normal operating conditions.^[57e907ec63549ebf:10]
