## Process overview

The triple extrusion process is a key step in producing cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) insulation for high-voltage cables. It applies three layers in one continuous extrusion: the conductor screen (a semi-conductive layer), the XLPE insulation, and the insulation screen (another semi-conductive layer). This single-pass approach eliminates interfaces between layers, which are potential sites for defects such as voids or contaminants that could initiate [[water.treeing]] or [[electrical.treeing]].^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:XLPE Insulation Material]

## Materials

The insulation layer uses a [[super.clean.xlpe.compound]] with an [[antioxidant.package]] to achieve long service life. The semi-conductive screens are typically made from carbon-black-filled polymers. The entire assembly is then cross-linked via [[peroxide.crosslinking]] during or immediately after extrusion.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:XLPE Insulation Material]

## Benefits

- **Reduced defect density**: By eliminating layer interfaces, the risk of partial discharge and treeing initiation is minimized.
- **Improved dielectric strength**: The homogeneous structure supports the high dielectric strength of XLPE (20–30 kV/mm).
- **Enhanced longevity**: Modern compounds with antioxidants achieve design lifetimes exceeding 40 years.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:XLPE Insulation Material]

## Applications

The triple extrusion process is standard for cables rated 66 kV and above, used in [[underground.urban.networks]], [[submarine.crossings]], and [[offshore.wind.farm.connections]].^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:XLPE Insulation Material]
