## Overview

The semi-conductive screen is an integral part of [[high_voltage_cable_systems]], particularly those using [[xlpe_insulation]]. It is applied in two locations within the cable core: an inner screen over the conductor and an outer screen over the insulation. Both layers are essential for the reliable operation of [[hv_cable_applications]] such as [[underground_urban_networks]], [[submarine_crossings]], and [[offshore_wind_farm_connections]].

## Function

The primary role of the semi-conductive screen is to control the electric field within the cable. By providing a smooth, conductive interface between the conductor and the insulation, it eliminates voids and irregularities that could cause localized electrical stress. This suppression of [[partial_discharge_measurement|partial discharges]] is critical for preventing long-term degradation of the [[xlpe_insulation_properties|XLPE insulation]]. The screen also acts as a barrier to moisture ingress and facilitates the bonding of the insulation to the conductor and the [[metallic_sheath]].

## Materials and Manufacturing

Semi-conductive screens are typically composed of a polymer matrix (often polyethylene or EVA) loaded with carbon black to achieve the required conductivity. The material is co-extruded with the XLPE insulation in a [[xlpe_manufacturing_process|triple extrusion process]], ensuring intimate contact and a defect-free interface. The quality of the screen material is governed by strict specifications, and suppliers such as [[borealis]] and [[dow_chemical]] provide approved compounds for HV cable applications. The screen must maintain its semi-conductive properties over the cable's design lifetime, resisting thermal aging and mechanical stress during installation.

## Installation and Testing

During [[hv_cable_installation_bending_radius|installation]], the semi-conductive screen must not be damaged or delaminated. Excessive bending or pulling tension can cause cracks or separation, leading to field enhancement and eventual failure. After installation, the integrity of the screen is verified through [[hv_cable_after_installation_testing|post-installation tests]] such as [[partial_discharge_measurement]] and [[sheath_integrity_test|sheath integrity testing]]. These tests ensure that the screen continues to perform its function under operating conditions.

## Standards and Quality

The design and testing of semi-conductive screens are covered by international standards such as [[iec_62067]]. Routine testing includes [[hv_cable_routine_testing|partial discharge measurement at 5 pC sensitivity]] and [[high_voltage_withstand_test|high voltage withstand tests]]. Approved suppliers for screen materials are listed in the [[approved_supplier_list_hv_cable_components|approved supplier list]], ensuring consistent quality across the supply chain.
