## Overview

The **semi-conductive screen** is a critical component of high-voltage cable systems, particularly those using cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) insulation. It consists of a thin layer of semi-conductive material applied directly over the conductor (conductor screen) and over the insulation (insulation screen). Its primary function is to ensure a uniform electric field distribution within the cable core, preventing partial discharges and electrical treeing that could lead to premature failure.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:1]

## Construction and Materials

The semi-conductive screen is typically co-extruded with the XLPE insulation in a triple extrusion process, ensuring intimate contact and void-free interfaces. Common materials include carbon-black-filled polymers such as Borealis Visico LE4253, Dow Chemical HFDA-4202 EC, and ExxonMobil HDPE compound.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:1] The screen must have a controlled resistivity (typically in the range of 10²–10⁵ Ω·cm) to provide adequate conductivity for field grading while maintaining sufficient resistivity to avoid excessive leakage currents.

## Functions

- **Electric field grading**: Smoothes the electric field at the conductor–insulation and insulation–metallic sheath interfaces, reducing stress concentrations.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:1]
- **Partial discharge suppression**: Eliminates cavities and irregularities that could initiate partial discharges.
- **Water treeing mitigation**: Provides a barrier against moisture ingress that can cause water treeing in XLPE insulation.
- **Thermal stability**: Maintains electrical properties over the cable's operating temperature range (typically up to 90 °C continuous, 105 °C emergency).

## Testing and Quality Assurance

Semi-conductive screens are subject to rigorous testing as part of the cable system qualification. Key tests include:
- **Partial discharge measurement** (per IEC 62067) to verify void-free interfaces.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:1]
- **High-voltage withstand test** to confirm dielectric strength.
- **Resistivity measurement** to ensure the screen meets specified conductivity limits.

## Applications

Semi-conductive screens are used in all modern XLPE-insulated high-voltage cables for underground urban networks, submarine crossings, and offshore wind farm connections.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:1] They are essential for cables rated above 1 kV and are mandatory for cables operating at transmission voltages (≥ 66 kV).
