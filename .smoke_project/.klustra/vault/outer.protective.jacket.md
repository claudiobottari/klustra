The outer protective jacket is the final layer of a high-voltage cable system, surrounding the metallic sheath and other internal components. It serves as a barrier against moisture, chemicals, and physical abrasion, ensuring the cable's integrity during installation and operation. As specified in standard cable construction, the outer protective jacket is part of the cable core, which also includes the conductor, semi-conductive screen, XLPE insulation, and metallic sheath.^[8085135b0a96974f:narrative] The jacket is typically made from materials such as high-density polyethylene (HDPE) or other polymers, offering flexibility and durability. Its design is critical for applications like [[underground.urban.networks]], [[submarine.crossings]], and [[offshore.wind.farm.connections]], where cables must withstand harsh conditions. Proper handling during installation, including adherence to [[bending.radii]] and [[installation.minimum.bending.radius]] constraints, is essential to avoid damage to the jacket.
