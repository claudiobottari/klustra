## Overview

**Duct installation** is a method of laying power cables inside pre-installed underground conduits, providing mechanical protection and future cable replacement capability. It is commonly used in [[underground_urban_networks]], but also applied in [[submarine_crossings]] and [[offshore_wind_farms]] where ducts are embedded in the seabed.

## Installation procedure

Before pulling, all ducts must be cleaned and inspected to remove debris, sharp edges, or obstructions that could damage the cable sheath.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Duct Installation] A [[cable_lubricant]] compatible with the outer sheath material is applied to reduce friction during installation.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Duct Installation]

A [[cable_winch_tension_monitoring]] system is used to pull the cable at a steady rate of **5–10 m/min**.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Duct Installation] The [[minimum_bending_radius]] during installation must be at least **20× the cable outer diameter**; the [[maximum_pulling_tension]] must not exceed **50 N/mm²** on the conductor.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:General Requirements] The [[installation_temperature]] must be **above 0 °C** to prevent sheath stiffening.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:General Requirements]

## Design considerations

Duct installation is preferred in environments where future cable replacement or maintenance is anticipated. It offers better protection against mechanical damage compared to [[direct_burial]], but requires a higher initial investment in conduit infrastructure. Duct routes must account for pulling tension limits and minimum bending radii; excessive bends or long sections may require intermediate pulling points or installation of [[prefabricated_joints]] within [[joint_bay_dimensions]].^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Jointing]
