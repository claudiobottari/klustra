# Routine testing

Routine testing is a set of mandatory electrical tests performed on every manufactured length of [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] cable after production. These tests verify that the cable meets the design specifications and is free from manufacturing defects prior to shipment. The tests are defined in the [[IEC 62067]] standard and are part of the broader cable testing framework that also includes [[type_testing]] and [[after_installation_testing]].^[dc426de07f1561d5:section:Cable Testing Standards]

## Required tests

Every manufactured cable length undergoes the following routine tests:^[dc426de07f1561d5:subsection:Routine Testing]

1. **[[high_voltage_withstand_test]]** – a high-voltage withstand test performed at 2.5 times the rated voltage (U0) for 30 minutes.
2. **[[partial_discharge_measurement]]** – partial discharge measurement with a threshold of less than 5 pC.
3. **[[conductor_resistance_check]]** – conductor resistance check.

These tests are conducted in the factory before the cable is shipped. The results are recorded to confirm compliance with the design lifetime and material specifications.^[dc426de07f1561d5:section:Cable Testing Standards]
