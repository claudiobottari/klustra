## local.electrical.safety.regulations

**Local electrical safety regulations** are statutory requirements that govern the safe installation, operation, and maintenance of high‑voltage (HV) cable systems. They complement international standards such as [[iec.61936-1]] and [[company.specific.safety.procedures]] to ensure a comprehensive safety framework.^[46d617e505500254:Regulatory Framework]

### Scope and Compliance

All HV cable work must comply with the local electrical safety regulations applicable in the jurisdiction where the work is performed. These regulations typically mandate specific safe working practices, including:

- [[cable.isolation.and.earthing]] of the cable at both ends before any work.^[46d617e505500254:Safe Working Practices]
- [[voltage.absence.verification]] using approved indicators.^[46d617e505500254:Safe Working Practices]
- Application of [[safety.locks.and.tags]].^[46d617e505500254:Safe Working Practices]
- Establishment of a [[safe.work.zone.with.barriers]].^[46d617e505500254:Safe Working Practices]

### Emergency Procedures

In the event of a cable fault during live operation, local regulations (or associated safety procedures) prescribe:

- Maintaining an [[exclusion.zone.for.cable.fault]] of 3 m around the suspected fault location.^[46d617e505500254:Emergency Procedures]
- [[fault.re-energization.prohibition]] – not re‑energizing without inspection.^[46d617e505500254:Emergency Procedures]
- [[control.centre.reporting]] – immediate reporting to the control centre.^[46d617e505500254:Emergency Procedures]

### Variation by Jurisdiction

The exact content of local electrical safety regulations varies between countries and regions. Operators must always consult the specific regulations applicable to their work site, in addition to the international standard [[iec.61936-1]] and their own [[company.specific.safety.procedures]].^[46d617e505500254:Regulatory Framework]
