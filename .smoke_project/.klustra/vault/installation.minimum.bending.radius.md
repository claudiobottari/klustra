## Installation Minimum Bending Radius

The **installation minimum bending radius** is a critical parameter that defines the smallest radius a [[high-voltage.cable.systems|high‑voltage cable]] may be bent during pulling, laying, or handling without risking damage to the conductor, insulation, or sheath. For generic high‑voltage cable installations, the minimum bending radius is specified as **20 times the cable outer diameter**.^[8913d160a82c281a:General Requirements]

This value is a basic requirement during installation and must be respected regardless of the installation method (e.g., [[installation.duct.installation|duct installation]], [[installation.direct.burial|direct burial]]). The actual allowable radius may be influenced by the cable construction – including the type of [[xlpe.insulation|insulation]], [[copper.conductor|conductor material]], [[metallic.sheath|metallic sheath]], and [[outer.protective.jacket|outer jacket]] – but the 20× diameter rule is a common starting point for many installations. 

Practical approval of the bending radius should be verified against the manufacturer’s data sheet and the specific cable design. Exceeding the minimum bending radius can cause permanent deformation, cracking of the [[semi-conductive.screen|semi‑conductive screen]], or damage to the [[metallic.sheath|metallic sheath]], which may lead to premature failure under operating conditions.
