# Routine Testing of High Voltage Cables

Routine testing is performed on every manufactured length of [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] to verify that the cable meets design specifications and is free from manufacturing defects. These tests are distinct from [[hv_cable_type_testing]] (which validates the system design) and [[hv_cable_after_installation_testing]] (which verifies installation integrity).

## Required Tests

Every cable length must undergo the following routine tests:

1. **High-voltage withstand test** – The cable is subjected to 2.5 times the rated voltage (U₀) for 30 minutes to confirm insulation strength.^[362871cd3841da0e:1]
2. **Partial discharge measurement** – Partial discharge activity is measured with a threshold of less than 5 pC to detect internal voids or defects in the [[xlpe_insulation]].^[362871cd3841da0e:1]
3. **Conductor resistance check** – The electrical resistance of the conductor is measured to ensure it meets the specified value, confirming material quality and cross-sectional area.^[362871cd3841da0e:1]

## Purpose and Scope

Routine testing is a mandatory quality control step for all manufactured cable lengths. It ensures that the cable can withstand normal operating and transient overvoltages without failure, and that the insulation system (typically [[xlpe_insulation]]) is free from harmful partial discharge. The conductor resistance check verifies that the conductor (copper or aluminium) meets the required conductivity and that no manufacturing errors (e.g., broken strands) are present.

## Relation to Other Testing Stages

- **Type testing** (per [[iec_62067]]) validates the cable system design through electrical, mechanical, and thermal tests on a sample.^[362871cd3841da0e:1]
- **Routine testing** is applied to every production length, not just a sample.
- **After-installation testing** includes DC or VLF withstand, sheath integrity (10 kV DC for 1 minute), and joint resistance measurement.^[362871cd3841da0e:1]
