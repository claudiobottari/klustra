## Properties

XLPE insulation exhibits a dielectric strength of 20–30 kV/mm and can operate continuously at temperatures up to 90 °C, with an emergency rating of 130 °C for short durations. Its dielectric loss is low, with tan δ < 0.001.^[57e907ec63549ebf]

## Manufacturing Process

XLPE insulation is produced by [[peroxide_crosslinking]] during extrusion. The [[triple_extrusion_process]] applies the conductor screen, insulation, and insulation screen in a single pass to eliminate interfaces.^[57e907ec63549ebf]

## Degradation Mechanisms

[[Water_treeing]] and [[electrical_treeing]] are the primary aging mechanisms. Modern [[super_clean_xlpe_compounds]] with antioxidant packages achieve design lifetimes exceeding 40 years.^[57e907ec63549ebf]
