## Overview

**XLPE insulation** (cross-linked polyethylene) is the primary insulating material in modern [[high-voltage.cable.systems]] for voltages up to 500 kV.^[8085135b0a96974f:narrative] It is applied as a continuous layer around the conductor and is chemically cross-linked to improve thermal and electrical performance.

## Construction

In a typical HV cable, the core consists of a [[copper.conductor]] or [[aluminium.conductor]] surrounded by:
- a [[semi-conductive.screen]]
- the **XLPE insulation layer**
- a [[metallic.sheath]] for water blocking
- an [[outer.protective.jacket]]^[8085135b0a96974f:narrative]

The insulation is produced via a [[triple.extrusion.process]] and [[peroxide.crosslinking]] to achieve the required dielectric strength and resistance to [[water.treeing]] and [[electrical.treeing]].

## Applications

XLPE-insulated cables are deployed in [[underground.urban.networks]], [[submarine.crossings]], and [[offshore.wind.farm.connections]].^[8085135b0a96974f:narrative]

## Installation Considerations

Installation of XLPE-insulated cables requires careful attention to [[bending.radii]] and thermal management to avoid damage to the insulation.^[8085135b0a96974f:narrative] Specific parameters such as [[installation.minimum.bending.radius]], [[installation.maximum.pulling.tension]], and [[installation.temperature]] must be observed during pulling and laying.
