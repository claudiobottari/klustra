## Overview

**Joint resistance measurement** is an after-installation test performed on high-voltage cable systems to verify the electrical continuity and quality of cable joints. It is part of the commissioning tests required by standards such as IEC 62067 and is typically conducted alongside DC or VLF withstand testing and sheath integrity testing.^[dc426de07f1561d5:testing_procedures.md]

## Purpose

The primary purpose of joint resistance measurement is to detect high-resistance connections or poor crimping in [[prefabricated_joints]] or field-assembled joints. Elevated resistance at a joint can lead to localised heating, accelerated ageing of [[xlpe_insulation_material]], and eventual failure of the cable system.^[dc426de07f1561d5:testing_procedures.md]

## Procedure

Joint resistance measurement is performed after the cable has been installed and jointed, but before the system is put into service. The test is carried out using a low-resistance ohmmeter (micro-ohmmeter) or a Kelvin bridge. The measurement is taken between the [[conductor_resistance_check]] points on either side of the joint, and the result is compared with the resistance of an equivalent length of continuous conductor.^[dc426de07f1561d5:testing_procedures.md]

Typical acceptance criteria:
- The measured joint resistance should not exceed the resistance of an equivalent length of conductor by more than 5% (or as specified by the manufacturer).
- For [[prefabricated_joints]], the manufacturer's recommended values are used.^[dc426de07f1561d5:testing_procedures.md]

## Relation to Other Tests

Joint resistance measurement is one of several after-installation tests specified in IEC 62067. It is performed after the [[high_voltage_withstand_test]] and [[partial_discharge_measurement]] have been completed, and before the [[sheath_integrity_test]]. The test is also referenced in company-specific safety procedures and local electrical safety regulations.^[dc426de07f1561d5:testing_procedures.md]

## Equipment

- Low-resistance ohmmeter (micro-ohmmeter) with a resolution of at least 0.1 μΩ
- Kelvin bridge for four-wire measurement
- Test leads with crocodile clips or Kelvin clips
- Calibration certificate for the measuring instrument^[dc426de07f1561d5:testing_procedures.md]

## Safety Considerations

Before performing joint resistance measurement, the cable must be isolated and earthed in accordance with [[cable_isolation_and_earthing]] procedures. The test area must be designated as a [[safe_work_zone_with_barriers]], and [[voltage_absence_verification]] must be carried out. All personnel must follow [[company_specific_safety_procedures]] and [[local_electrical_safety_regulations]].^[dc426de07f1561d5:testing_procedures.md]
