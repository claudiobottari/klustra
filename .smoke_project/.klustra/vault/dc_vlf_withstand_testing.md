DC/VLF withstand testing is an on-site commissioning test for high-voltage cable systems, performed after installation to verify the integrity of the insulation system. It is one of several after-installation tests, alongside sheath integrity testing and joint resistance measurement.^[dc426de07f1561d5:testing_procedures.md]

The test applies a high voltage—either direct current (DC) or very low frequency (VLF)—to the cable conductor for a specified duration. The purpose is to stress the insulation in a manner that reveals defects or weaknesses that could lead to failure in service. The specific voltage levels and test durations are defined by relevant standards and project specifications.

## Relationship to Other Testing

DC/VLF withstand testing is distinct from type testing and routine testing. Type tests (per IEC 62067) validate the cable system design through electrical, mechanical, and thermal tests. Routine tests are performed on every manufactured cable length and include a high-voltage withstand test at 2.5 U0 for 30 minutes, partial discharge measurement (threshold < 5 pC), and conductor resistance check. After-installation testing, including DC/VLF withstand testing, is performed on-site to confirm that the cable system has not been damaged during transport, handling, or installation.^[dc426de07f1561d5:testing_procedures.md]

## Application

DC/VLF withstand testing is commonly applied to [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] with [[cross_linked_polyethylene_insulation]] (XLPE). It is used in various installations including [[underground_urban_networks]], [[submarine_crossings]], and [[offshore_wind_farms]]. The test is part of the commissioning process to ensure the cable system meets the required [[dielectric_strength]] and is free from defects such as [[water_treeing]] or [[electrical_treeing]] that could compromise the [[design_lifetime]].

## Safety Considerations

Before performing DC/VLF withstand testing, strict safety procedures must be followed. These include [[cable_isolation_and_earthing]], [[voltage_absence_verification]], and the use of [[safety_locks_and_tags]]. A [[safe_work_zone_with_barriers]] must be established, and a [[fault_exclusion_zone]] defined. Personnel must be familiar with [[local_electrical_safety_regulations]] and [[company_specific_safety_procedures]], as well as the [[cable_fault_emergency_procedure]].
