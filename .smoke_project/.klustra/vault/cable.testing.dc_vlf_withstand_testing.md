## DC VLF Withstand Testing

DC VLF (very low frequency) withstand testing is a type of [[cable.testing.after_installation_testing]] used to verify the insulation integrity of [[high-voltage.cable.systems]] after installation. It is listed as one of the on‑site commissioning tests in relevant standards, alongside the [[cable.testing.sheath_integrity_test]] and [[cable.testing.joint_resistance_measurement]].^[362871cd3841da0e:After-Installation Testing]

The test applies a DC or VLF voltage to the cable to stress the insulation and detect latent defects. The choice between DC or VLF depends on the cable type and installation conditions. The test is typically performed after the cable has been installed, jointed, and terminated, and before it is put into service.
