**Company-specific safety procedures** are an integral part of the regulatory framework for high-voltage (HV) cable work. All HV cable installations must comply with these procedures in addition to IEC 61936-1 and local electrical safety regulations^[b359cf61b264db52]. While general safe working practices—such as isolation and earthing, voltage absence verification, application of safety locks and tags, and establishment of safe work zones with barriers—are commonly required, company-specific procedures may impose additional or more stringent requirements tailored to the organization’s equipment, environment, or operational history^[b359cf61b264db52]. These procedures also extend to emergency protocols for cable faults, including maintaining a 3 m exclusion zone, prohibiting re-energization without inspection, and immediate reporting to the control centre^[b359cf61b264db52].
