## Definition

A **fault exclusion zone** is a designated safety perimeter established around a suspected or confirmed fault location on a high-voltage cable system. Its purpose is to protect personnel from electrical hazards, including step and touch potentials, arc flash, and unexpected re-energization, during fault investigation and repair operations.

## Regulatory Basis

The establishment of a fault exclusion zone is part of the safety framework defined in [[iec_61936_1]] (Power installations exceeding 1 kV), supplemented by [[local_electrical_safety_regulations]] and [[company_specific_safety_procedures]].^[b359cf61b264db52:1]

## Dimensions and Marking

During live operation, a minimum exclusion zone of **3 m** must be maintained around the suspected fault location.^[b359cf61b264db52:2] The zone should be clearly marked with barriers, warning signs, and, where necessary, safety observers. The exact radius may be increased based on soil resistivity, fault current magnitude, and local regulations.

## Procedures

When a cable fault occurs during live operation:
1. Immediately establish the exclusion zone and prevent unauthorized access.^[b359cf61b264db52:2]
2. Do not attempt to re-energize the cable without inspection.^[b359cf61b264db52:2]
3. Report the incident to the control centre without delay.^[b359cf61b264db52:2]

Before any work on the cable system (including fault location and repair), the following safe working practices must be completed:
- Isolate and earth the cable at both ends ([[cable_isolation_and_earthing]]).^[b359cf61b264db52:1]
- Verify absence of voltage using approved indicators ([[voltage_absence_verification]]).^[b359cf61b264db52:1]
- Apply safety locks and tags ([[safety_locks_and_tags]]).^[b359cf61b264db52:1]
- Establish a safe work zone with barriers ([[safe_work_zone_with_barriers]]).^[b359cf61b264db52:1]
