## joint_bay_dimensions

A **joint bay** is a specially excavated pit or trench section that provides the necessary space for [[jointing]] high-voltage cables, particularly in [[direct_burial]] or [[duct_installation]] systems. The bay must be large enough to accommodate the cable ends, the jointing equipment, and the personnel working in a controlled environment, especially when using [[prefabricated_joints]].

### Dimensions

According to standard installation practices, the minimum dimensions for a joint bay are **3 m in length by 1.5 m in width**^[4548a58ab1ab402c:installation_guide.txt]. These dimensions ensure adequate working space for cable preparation, joint assembly, and testing. The specific size may be adjusted based on the cable voltage class, number of circuits, and local regulations, but the 3 m × 1.5 m minimum is a common baseline.

### Related requirements

- The joint bay should be kept dry and clean, often with a waterproof cover or tent, to maintain the controlled environment required for reliable jointing.
- The bay depth is typically governed by the cable trench depth; for direct burial, the minimum trench depth is 800 mm in urban areas and 1200 mm in rural areas^[4548a58ab1ab402c:installation_guide.txt], with the joint bay floor excavated to the same level.
- After installation, the bay is backfilled with compacted material, and the cable is protected by a sand bedding layer and warning tape, as per standard direct burial practice.
