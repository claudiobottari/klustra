## Overview

Jointing is the process of connecting two or more cable sections to form a continuous electrical path, typically used in [[high_voltage_cable_systems]]. It is a critical operation that requires controlled environmental conditions, specialized components, and rigorous testing to ensure long-term reliability.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:jointing]

## Installation Requirements

All joints must be installed in a controlled environment to prevent contamination and ensure proper curing of materials.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:jointing] [[Prefabricated_joints]] are preferred for faster installation, as they reduce on-site assembly time and minimize the risk of installation errors.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:jointing] Joint bays require minimum dimensions of 3 m × 1.5 m to provide adequate working space for personnel and equipment.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:jointing]

## Components and Materials

Modern jointing systems for [[cross_linked_polyethylene_insulation]] cables typically include [[prefabricated_joints]] that incorporate [[semi_conductive_screen]] stress control, [[metallic_sheath]] continuity, and [[outer_protective_jacket]] sealing. Common commercial products include [[prysmian_pre_moulded_joints_and_terminations]] and [[nkt_cold_shrink_joints]].^[4548a58ab1ab402c:jointing] The jointing process must be compatible with the cable's [[xlpe_insulation_material]] and its [[dielectric_strength]] and [[operating_temperature]] ratings.

## Testing and Quality Assurance

After installation, joints must undergo a series of tests to verify integrity and performance. These include [[after_installation_testing]] such as [[high_voltage_withstand_test]], [[partial_discharge_measurement]], [[conductor_resistance_check]], [[dc_vlf_withstand_testing]], [[sheath_integrity_test]], and [[joint_resistance_measurement]].^[4548a58ab1ab402c:jointing] Type testing and routine testing are performed according to [[iec_62067]] to ensure compliance with international standards.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:jointing]

## Safety Considerations

Jointing operations must follow [[local_electrical_safety_regulations]] and [[company_specific_safety_procedures]]. Key safety steps include [[cable_isolation_and_earthing]], [[voltage_absence_verification]], use of [[safety_locks_and_tags]], and establishing a [[safe_work_zone_with_barriers]].^[4548a58ab1ab402c:jointing] In the event of a cable fault, a [[cable_fault_emergency_procedure]] must be in place, and a [[fault_exclusion_zone]] should be defined.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:jointing]

## Applications

Jointing is essential in [[underground_urban_networks]], [[submarine_crossings]], and [[offshore_wind_farms]] where long cable runs require multiple sections.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:jointing] The design lifetime of a properly installed joint matches that of the cable system, typically 30–40 years.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:jointing]
