## After-Installation Testing

After-installation testing, also known as commissioning testing, is performed on-site after a cable system has been installed. Its purpose is to verify that the cable, joints, and terminations have not been damaged during installation and that the system is ready for energisation. The following tests are typically carried out:^[362871cd3841da0e:Cable Testing Standards]

- **DC or VLF withstand testing** – A high voltage is applied to the cable to check for insulation defects. See [[cable.testing.dc_vlf_withstand_testing]].
- **Sheath integrity test** – A DC voltage of 10 kV is applied for 1 minute to verify the integrity of the outer sheath. See [[cable.testing.sheath_integrity_test]].
- **Joint resistance measurement** – The resistance of cable joints is measured to ensure low, stable electrical connections. See [[cable.testing.joint_resistance_measurement]].

These on-site tests complement the [[cable.testing.type_testing]] and [[cable.testing.routine_testing]] performed during manufacturing.^[362871cd3841da0e:Cable Testing Standards]
