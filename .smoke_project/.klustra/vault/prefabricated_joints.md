## Overview

**Prefabricated joints** are factory-assembled cable accessories used to connect two lengths of high-voltage power cable. They are designed to be installed in the field with minimal on-site assembly, reducing installation time and the risk of contamination or human error. Prefabricated joints are widely used in [[underground_urban_networks]], [[submarine_crossings]], and [[offshore_wind_farms]] where reliability and speed of installation are critical.

## Design and construction

A prefabricated joint typically consists of a [[cable_core]] connector, [[semi_conductive_screen]] stress control elements, [[cross_linked_polyethylene_insulation]] (XLPE) or other insulation, a [[metallic_sheath]] for electrical continuity and moisture barrier, and an [[outer_protective_jacket]]. The insulation is often based on [[xlpe_insulation_material]] compounds such as [[borealis_visico_le4253]], [[dow_chemical_hfda_4202_ec]], or [[exxonmobil_hdpe_compound]]; the metallic sheath may incorporate [[arkema_aluminium_pe_laminate_tape]] for enhanced moisture protection.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]

Key performance parameters include [[dielectric_strength]], [[operating_temperature]] (typically up to 90 °C continuous, 105 °C emergency), and [[dielectric_loss]]. The insulation is produced via [[peroxide_crosslinking]] and [[triple_extrusion_process]] to achieve high purity and reduce defects such as [[water_treeing]] and [[electrical_treeing]]. Modern designs use [[super_clean_xlpe_compounds]] to extend the [[design_lifetime]] beyond 40 years.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]

## Installation requirements

Installation of prefabricated joints must be performed in a controlled environment to avoid moisture and dust contamination.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0] The following general requirements apply:

- **Minimum bending radius**: 20× cable outer diameter during installation.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- **Maximum pulling tension**: 50 N/mm² on the conductor.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- **Installation temperature**: above 0 °C.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]

### Joint bay dimensions

Joint bays must have minimum dimensions of 3 m × 1.5 m to allow safe access and proper alignment of the cables.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]

### Duct installation

When cables are pulled into ducts, the following steps are required:
- Clean and inspect all ducts before pulling.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- Apply [[cable_lubricant]] compatible with the outer sheath material.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- Use a [[cable_winch_tension_monitoring]] system.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- Pull at a steady rate of 5–10 m/min.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]

### Direct burial

For direct burial installations:
- Trench depth: minimum 800 mm in urban areas, 1200 mm in rural areas.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- [[sand_bedding]] layer of 100 mm below and above the cable.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- [[warning_tape]] placed 300 mm above the cable.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- [[backfill]] with compacted excavated material.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]

## Testing and standards

Prefabricated joints must comply with [[iec_62067]] for power cables with rated voltages above 150 kV up to 500 kV. Testing includes:
- [[type_testing]] (design verification).^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- [[routine_testing]] (factory tests on every joint).^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- [[after_installation_testing]] (field tests).^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]

Field tests typically comprise:
- [[high_voltage_withstand_test]] (AC or DC).^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- [[partial_discharge_measurement]].^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- [[conductor_resistance_check]].^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- [[dc_vlf_withstand_testing]] (very low frequency).^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- [[sheath_integrity_test]].^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
- [[joint_resistance_measurement]].^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]

Additionally, installation must follow [[iec_61936_1]], [[local_electrical_safety_regulations]], and [[company_specific_safety_procedures]]. Safety measures include [[cable_isolation_and_earthing]], [[voltage_absence_verification]], [[safety_locks_and_tags]], [[safe_work_zone_with_barriers]], and establishing a [[fault_exclusion_zone]]. A [[cable_fault_emergency_procedure]] must be in place.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:0-0]
