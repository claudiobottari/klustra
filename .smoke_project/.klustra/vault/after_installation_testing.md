## Purpose

After-installation testing ensures that the cable system has been correctly installed and that no defects were introduced during handling, pulling, jointing, or termination. It is a mandatory step before energising the circuit and is typically governed by standards such as [[iec_62067]] and local regulations.

## Tests performed

On-site commissioning tests for high-voltage cable systems include the following:

- **DC or VLF withstand testing** – A high-voltage withstand test using direct current (DC) or very low frequency (VLF) voltage to stress the insulation and detect gross defects. This is a form of [[high_voltage_withstand_test]] and is often referred to as [[dc_vlf_withstand_testing]].^[dc426de07f1561d5:narrative]
- **Sheath integrity test** – A 10 kV DC voltage is applied for 1 minute to verify the integrity of the metallic sheath or screen. This test is known as [[sheath_integrity_test]].^[dc426de07f1561d5:narrative]
- **Joint resistance measurement** – The resistance of each joint is measured to confirm low-resistance, stable connections. This is [[joint_resistance_measurement]].^[dc426de07f1561d5:narrative]

These tests complement the routine factory tests (e.g., [[partial_discharge_measurement]], [[conductor_resistance_check]]) and are performed after all installation activities, including [[jointing]] and termination, are complete.

## Relation to other testing stages

- **Type testing** (per [[iec_62067]]) validates the cable system design through electrical, mechanical, and thermal tests.^[dc426de07f1561d5:narrative]
- **Routine testing** is performed on every manufactured cable length and includes a high-voltage withstand test at 2.5 U₀ for 30 minutes, partial discharge measurement (< 5 pC), and conductor resistance check.^[dc426de07f1561d5:narrative]
- **After-installation testing** is the final on-site verification before commissioning.
