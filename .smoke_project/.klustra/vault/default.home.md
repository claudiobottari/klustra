Welcome to the **High-Voltage Cable Engineering Knowledge Base** — your central resource for understanding the design, installation, testing, and safe operation of high-voltage cable systems used in power transmission networks up to 500 kV. This domain covers the full lifecycle of cable assets, from raw materials and manufacturing through to in-service degradation and failure analysis, while placing equal emphasis on the critical safety procedures that protect personnel working with these dangerous systems.

Begin your exploration with the [[default.cluster.l1.high-voltage-cable-systems]] cluster, which dives into the engineering of XLPE-insulated cables, including conductor design, insulation materials, armoring, and accessories. Here you will find detailed guidance on cable installation techniques (trenching, direct burial, ducting, and pulling), factory and field testing methods, and common degradation mechanisms such as water treeing, partial discharge, and thermal runaway. This cluster is the core technical reference for any engineer involved in cable specification, procurement, construction, or asset management.

Equally important is the [[default.cluster.l1.hv-cable-safety-procedures]] cluster, which provides a comprehensive framework for working safely on high-voltage cables. Topics include statutory regulations, isolation and earthing procedures, voltage detection and testing, and emergency response protocols for cable faults. This cluster is essential reading for all field personnel, maintenance teams, and safety officers who must ensure that every task—from routine switching to fault repair—is performed without risk to life or equipment.

Together, these two clusters form a complete body of knowledge that bridges the gap between theoretical design principles and practical, safe execution. Whether you are a new engineer seeking foundational understanding or an experienced specialist looking for best-practice references, this knowledge base is structured to help you navigate seamlessly from concept to application. Use the links above to explore each area in depth, and refer to the tag system for cross-cutting topics that span multiple domains.
