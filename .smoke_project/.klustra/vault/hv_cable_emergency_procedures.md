## Overview

**HV cable emergency procedures** are a set of protocols and actions to be followed when a fault or incident occurs on a [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] during live operation. These procedures are part of the broader [[hv_cable_safety_standards]] and [[hv_cable_regulatory_framework]], and are designed to protect personnel, the public, and equipment.

## Key Emergency Actions

In the event of a cable fault during live operation, the following immediate actions must be taken:

1. **Maintain an exclusion zone** of at least 3 m around the suspected fault location. No personnel should enter this zone until the situation is assessed.^[46d617e505500254:1]
2. **Do not attempt to re-energize** the cable without a thorough inspection and clearance from the responsible authority.^[46d617e505500254:1]
3. **Report the incident immediately** to the control centre or designated supervisor.^[46d617e505500254:1]

## Relationship to Safe Working Practices

Emergency procedures are closely linked to [[hv_cable_safe_working_practices]]. Before any work on cable systems, standard safe practices include:
- Isolating and earthing the cable at both ends
- Verifying the absence of voltage using approved indicators
- Applying safety locks and tags
- Establishing a safe work zone with barriers^[46d617e505500254:1]

These preparatory steps reduce the likelihood of emergencies and ensure that if a fault occurs, the response is structured and safe.

## Regulatory Context

All HV cable work, including emergency response, must comply with:
- IEC 61936‑1 (Power installations exceeding 1 kV)
- Local electrical safety regulations
- Company-specific safety procedures^[46d617e505500254:1]
