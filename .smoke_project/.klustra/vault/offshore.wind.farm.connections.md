## Overview

Offshore wind farm connections rely on high-voltage (HV) cable systems to transmit generated electricity to onshore substations. These connections are a critical application of [[high-voltage.cable.systems]] and typically use cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) insulation rated for voltages up to 500 kV.^[8085135b0a96974f]

## Key Components

The cable core for offshore wind farm connections consists of a [[copper.conductor]] or [[aluminium.conductor]] surrounded by:
- A [[semi-conductive.screen]]
- An [[xlpe.insulation]] layer
- A [[metallic.sheath]] for water blocking
- An [[outer.protective.jacket]]^[8085135b0a96974f]

## Installation Considerations

Deployment of these cables requires careful handling of [[bending.radii]] and thermal conditions to avoid damage during laying and operation.^[8085135b0a96974f] The cables are often installed via submarine crossings or buried in the seabed, following practices similar to [[submarine.crossings]] and [[installation.direct.burial]].

## Applications

HV cable systems for offshore wind farm connections are part of a broader set of applications that also include [[underground.urban.networks]] and [[submarine.crossings]].^[8085135b0a96974f]
