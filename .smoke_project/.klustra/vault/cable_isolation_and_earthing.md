## Overview

**Cable isolation and earthing** refers to the mandatory safety procedures applied before any work on [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] to ensure that the cable is de‑energised and safely discharged. The process is a critical step in preventing electrical accidents during installation, maintenance, or repair.

## Regulatory Framework

All HV cable work must comply with the following standards and regulations:

- [[iec_61936_1]] (Power installations exceeding 1 kV)
- [[local_electrical_safety_regulations]]
- [[company_specific_safety_procedures]]

^[b359cf61b264db52:Regulatory Framework]

## Safe Working Practices

Before any work on cable systems, the following sequence of actions must be performed:

1. **Isolate and earth the cable at both ends** – This ensures no voltage can be present and that any stored charge is safely discharged.^[b359cf61b264db52:Safe Working Practices]
2. **Verify absence of voltage** using approved indicators ([[voltage_absence_verification]]).^[b359cf61b264db52:Safe Working Practices]
3. **Apply safety locks and tags** ([[safety_locks_and_tags]]) to prevent inadvertent re‑energisation.^[b359cf61b264db52:Safe Working Practices]
4. **Establish a safe work zone with barriers** ([[safe_work_zone_with_barriers]]) to protect personnel and the public.^[b359cf61b264db52:Safe Working Practices]

## Emergency Procedures

In the event of a cable fault during live operation, the following emergency response must be followed ([[cable_fault_emergency_procedure]]):

- Maintain an **exclusion zone of 3 m** around the suspected fault location.^[b359cf61b264db52:Emergency Procedures]
- **Do not attempt to re‑energise** without inspection.^[b359cf61b264db52:Emergency Procedures]
- **Report immediately** to the control centre.^[b359cf61b264db52:Emergency Procedures]
