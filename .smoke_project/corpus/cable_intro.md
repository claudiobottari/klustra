# High-Voltage Cable Systems

High-voltage (HV) cable systems are critical infrastructure for power transmission.
Modern HV cables use cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) insulation for voltages up to 500 kV.

## Key Components

The cable core consists of a copper or aluminium conductor surrounded by:
- Semi-conductive screen
- XLPE insulation layer
- Metallic sheath for water blocking
- Outer protective jacket

## Applications

HV cables are deployed in underground urban networks, submarine crossings,
and connections to offshore wind farms. Installation requires careful handling
of bending radii and thermal considerations.
