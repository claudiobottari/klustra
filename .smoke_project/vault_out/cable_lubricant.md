## Overview

A **cable lubricant** is a substance applied to the outer sheath of a cable to reduce friction during [[duct_installation]]. It is a critical component of the pulling process, as it protects the [[outer_protective_jacket]] from abrasion and ensures that the [[maximum_pulling_tension]] is not exceeded. The lubricant must be chemically compatible with the sheath material to avoid swelling, cracking, or other degradation.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:duct_installation]

## Application

During duct installation, the duct is first cleaned and inspected. The lubricant is then applied to the cable sheath, typically at the entry point of the duct or continuously via a lubricant pump. The cable is pulled at a steady rate of 5–10 m/min while a [[cable_winch_tension_monitoring]] system ensures the pulling tension stays below 50 N/mm² on the conductor.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:duct_installation]

## Compatibility

Lubricants are formulated for specific sheath materials, such as [[cross_linked_polyethylene_insulation]] or [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] jackets. Using an incompatible lubricant can damage the sheath, leading to moisture ingress or electrical failure. Manufacturers typically specify approved lubricants for their cables.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:duct_installation]
