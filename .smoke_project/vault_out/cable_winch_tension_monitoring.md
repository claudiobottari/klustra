## Overview

Cable winch tension monitoring is a critical safety and quality measure during the installation of [[high_voltage_cable_systems]], particularly when pulling cables through ducts. The winch must be fitted with a tension‑monitoring device that continuously displays the pulling force. The operator uses this information to keep the tension below the [[maximum_pulling_tension]] of 50 N/mm² on the conductor, thereby avoiding elongation or damage to the insulation and sheath layers.^[4548a58ab1ab402c]

## Application in duct installation

In [[duct_installation]], the cable is pulled using a winch at a steady rate of 5–10 m/min. A [[cable_lubricant]] compatible with the outer sheath material is applied to reduce friction. The tension monitor alerts the operator if the force approaches the limit, allowing immediate adjustment. This procedure is specified alongside other installation constraints such as [[minimum_bending_radius]] (20× cable outer diameter) and [[installation_temperature]] (above 0 °C).^[4548a58ab1ab402c]

## Related parameters

- [[maximum_pulling_tension]]: 50 N/mm² on the conductor
- [[minimum_bending_radius]]: 20× cable outer diameter
- [[installation_temperature]]: above 0 °C
- Pulling speed: 5–10 m/min
