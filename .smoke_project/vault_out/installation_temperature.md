## Installation Temperature

**Installation temperature** is a critical parameter for the safe and reliable installation of [[high_voltage_cable_systems]], particularly those with [[cross_linked_polyethylene_insulation]]. The minimum permissible installation temperature is **0 °C** (32 °F).^[4548a58ab1ab402c:General Requirements] This limit applies to the ambient air temperature as well as the temperature of the cable itself at the time of installation.

### Rationale

Below 0 °C, the [[cross_linked_polyethylene_insulation]] and the [[outer_protective_jacket]] become brittle and are susceptible to cracking or micro‑fracturing when subjected to bending or pulling forces. Even if the cable is stored at a higher temperature, exposure to sub‑zero ambient conditions during pulling can cause localised cooling and embrittlement. Therefore, installation must be scheduled or the work area heated to ensure the cable and its surroundings remain above the threshold throughout the operation.

### Related installation parameters

- [[minimum_bending_radius]]: 20× cable outer diameter during installation.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:General Requirements]
- [[maximum_pulling_tension]]: 50 N/mm² on the conductor.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:General Requirements]
- [[duct_installation]]: Pulling rate 5–10 m/min, with compatible [[cable_lubricant]] and [[cable_winch_tension_monitoring]].^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Duct Installation]
- [[direct_burial]]: Trench depth ≥800 mm (urban) / ≥1200 mm (rural), [[sand_bedding]] 100 mm below and above cable, [[warning_tape]] at 300 mm above cable.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Direct Burial]
- [[jointing]]: Prefabricated joints preferred; joint bay dimensions ≥3 m × 1.5 m; controlled environment required.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Jointing]
