**ExxonMobil HDPE compound** is a high-density polyethylene material approved for use as a [[sheath]] component in [[high_voltage_cable_systems]], specifically to provide a radial moisture barrier.^[f55e45b580b53352]

In the context of high-voltage cable construction, the compound is applied as a continuous layer over the [[metallic_sheath]] or directly over the [[cable_core]] to prevent water ingress, which can degrade insulation and lead to [[water_treeing]]. The material is designed to withstand the thermal and mechanical stresses encountered during cable installation and operation, including [[minimum_bending_radius]] and [[installation_temperature]] constraints.

== Approved supplier status ==
ExxonMobil is listed as an approved supplier of HDPE compound for radial moisture barriers in high-voltage cable systems.^[f55e45b580b53352] This designation implies that the compound meets the quality and performance requirements of cable manufacturers and system operators, often verified through [[type_testing]] and [[routine_testing]] in accordance with standards such as [[iec_62067]].

== Related components ==
The compound is part of a broader set of approved materials for high-voltage cables, including [[borealis_visico_le4253]] and [[dow_chemical_hfda_4202_ec]] for insulation, as well as [[arkema_aluminium_pe_laminate_tape]] for additional moisture protection.^[f55e45b580b53352] Accessories such as [[prysmian_pre_moulded_joints_and_terminations]] and [[nkt_cold_shrink_joints]] are also approved for use with cables incorporating this sheathing material.
