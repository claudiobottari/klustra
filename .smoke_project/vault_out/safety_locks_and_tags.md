## Overview

Safety locks and tags are physical devices used to secure isolation points on high-voltage cable systems, preventing accidental re-energization during maintenance or repair. They are a key element of safe working practices for [[high_voltage_cable_systems]], ensuring that workers are protected from electrical hazards.

## Regulatory Framework

All high-voltage cable work must comply with [[iec_61936_1]] (Power installations exceeding 1 kV), [[local_electrical_safety_regulations]], and [[company_specific_safety_procedures]].^[b359cf61b264db52:1-4]

## Safe Working Practices

Before any work on cable systems, the following steps must be taken:
1. Isolate and earth the cable at both ends ([[cable_isolation_and_earthing]]).
2. Verify absence of voltage using approved indicators ([[voltage_absence_verification]]).
3. Apply safety locks and tags ([[safety_locks_and_tags]]).
4. Establish safe work zone with barriers ([[safe_work_zone_with_barriers]]).^[b359cf61b264db52:6-10]

Safety locks and tags are applied after isolation and voltage verification to physically lock switches or disconnectors in the open position, with tags indicating the work being performed and the personnel responsible. This prevents unauthorized or accidental re-energization.

## Emergency Procedures

In case of a cable fault during live operation, an exclusion zone of 3 m must be maintained around the suspected fault location ([[fault_exclusion_zone]]). Do not attempt to re-energize without inspection, and report immediately to the control centre ([[cable_fault_emergency_procedure]]).^[b359cf61b264db52:12-15]
