The **design lifetime** of a high-voltage cable system is the period over which the cable is expected to operate reliably under specified conditions. For modern [[xlpe_insulation_material]] cables, design lifetimes exceeding 40 years are achievable through the use of super-clean compounds and advanced antioxidant packages^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material].

## Factors Influencing Design Lifetime

- **Material quality**: Super-clean XLPE compounds reduce the initiation of water treeing and electrical treeing, the primary aging mechanisms^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material].
- **Manufacturing process**: The triple-extrusion process eliminates interfaces that could become weak points^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material].
- **Operating conditions**: Continuous operation at up to 90°C and short-duration emergency ratings of 130°C are factored into lifetime predictions^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material].
- **Installation quality**: Proper handling, jointing, and testing per standards such as [[iec_62067]] and [[iec_61936_1]] are essential to achieve the design lifetime.

## Testing and Validation

Design lifetime is validated through type testing, routine testing, and after-installation testing, including high-voltage withstand tests, partial discharge measurement, and sheath integrity tests.

## Storia e revisioni

- Earlier XLPE compounds had shorter design lifetimes due to higher impurity levels and less effective antioxidant packages. The introduction of super-clean compounds extended the design lifetime beyond 40 years^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material].
