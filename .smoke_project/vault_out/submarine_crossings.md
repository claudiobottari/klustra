## Submarine Crossings

**Submarine crossings** are a specialized application of high-voltage cable systems used to transmit electrical power across bodies of water, such as rivers, lakes, or seas. They are a critical component of modern power infrastructure, enabling connections between land-based grids and offshore installations.

### Design and Components

Submarine crossings use cables with a construction similar to land-based high-voltage cables, but with enhanced protection against water ingress and mechanical stress. The cable core consists of a copper or aluminium conductor surrounded by:
- A semi-conductive screen
- Cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) insulation
- A metallic sheath for water blocking
- An outer protective jacket^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

The metallic sheath and outer jacket are designed to withstand the high hydrostatic pressure and abrasive conditions of the seabed.

### Applications

Submarine crossings are deployed in several key scenarios:
- **Underground urban networks**: Extending power grids across rivers or harbors within cities^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]
- **Offshore wind farms**: Connecting wind turbines to the onshore grid^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]
- **Interconnectors**: Linking the power grids of different countries or islands

### Installation Considerations

Installation of submarine crossings requires careful handling of bending radii and thermal considerations^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]. Key parameters include:
- [[minimum_bending_radius]]: The cable must not be bent beyond its specified limit during laying
- [[maximum_pulling_tension]]: Tension must be monitored to avoid damaging the cable core
- [[installation_temperature]]: The cable's flexibility and performance depend on ambient conditions

Specialized vessels and equipment are used to lay cables on the seabed, often burying them in a trench for protection against anchors and fishing gear.

### Testing and Quality Assurance

After installation, submarine crossings undergo rigorous testing to ensure integrity and performance. Tests include:
- [[high_voltage_withstand_test]]
- [[partial_discharge_measurement]]
- [[sheath_integrity_test]]
- [[dc_vlf_withstand_testing]]

These tests follow standards such as [[iec_62067]] and are critical for long-term reliability.
