# Super-clean XLPE compounds

**Super-clean XLPE compounds** are advanced formulations of [[cross_linked_polyethylene_insulation]] that incorporate optimised antioxidant packages and extreme-purity base polymers to suppress the initiation and growth of degradation phenomena—primarily [[water_treeing]] and [[electrical_treeing]]—in [[high_voltage_cable_systems]].  

## Composition and manufacture

The material is produced by [[peroxide_crosslinking]] during extrusion; a common production method is the [[triple_extrusion_process]], in which the conductor screen, insulation, and insulation screen are applied in a single pass to eliminate interfacial defects.  

Key ingredients include high-purity polyethylene, peroxide initiators, and proprietary antioxidant systems that scavenge free radicals generated during aging. The ‘super-clean’ designation refers to exceptionally low levels of ionic contaminants and micro-voids achieved through stringent process controls.  

## Performance characteristics

- **Dielectric strength:** 20–30 kV/mm  
- **Continuous operating temperature:** up to 90 °C  
- **Emergency rating:** 130 °C for short duration  
- **Low dielectric loss** (tan δ < 0.001)  

With these attributes, super-clean XLPE compounds enable a [[design_lifetime]] exceeding 40 years in typical service conditions.  

## Application in high-voltage cable systems

These compounds are the preferred insulation for cables operating at 66 kV and above, used in [[underground_urban_networks]], [[submarine_crossings]], and [[offshore_wind_farms]]. They form the primary insulation layer of the [[cable_core]], surrounded by [[semi_conductive_screen]] layers and a [[metallic_sheath]] before the [[outer_protective_jacket]].  

## Testing and qualification

Cables employing super-clean XLPE must pass [[type_testing]], [[routine_testing]], and [[after_installation_testing]] per standards such as [[iec_62067]]. Key tests include [[high_voltage_withstand_test]], [[partial_discharge_measurement]], [[conductor_resistance_check]], [[dc_vlf_withstand_testing]], [[sheath_integrity_test]], and [[joint_resistance_measurement]].
