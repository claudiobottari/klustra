## Overview

Direct burial is a cable installation technique where cables are laid directly into a trench and covered with backfill, without intermediate ducting. It is widely employed for [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] in [[underground_urban_networks]], [[submarine_crossings]], and [[offshore_wind_farms]] due to its cost-effectiveness and simplicity.

## Installation Requirements

### Trench Depth
The trench depth must be a minimum of 800 mm in urban areas and 1200 mm in rural areas to protect the cable from mechanical damage and environmental factors.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Direct Burial]

### Sand Bedding
A sand bedding layer of 100 mm is required both below and above the cable to provide a uniform support and reduce the risk of damage from sharp objects in the backfill.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Direct Burial]

### Warning Tape
A warning tape must be placed 300 mm above the cable to alert future excavation activities of the presence of buried cables.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Direct Burial]

### Backfill
The trench is backfilled with compacted excavated material, ensuring proper compaction to prevent settling and provide mechanical protection.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Direct Burial]

## General Installation Parameters

During installation, the following parameters must be observed:
- **Minimum bending radius**: 20 times the cable outer diameter.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:General Requirements]
- **Maximum pulling tension**: 50 N/mm² on the conductor.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:General Requirements]
- **Installation temperature**: Above 0 degrees Celsius.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:General Requirements]

## Jointing

All joints must be installed in a controlled environment to ensure quality and reliability. Prefabricated joints are preferred for faster installation. Joint bays require minimum dimensions of 3 m x 1.5 m to accommodate the jointing process.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Jointing]

## Related Components

Direct burial cables typically include a [[cable_core]] with [[cross_linked_polyethylene_insulation]], [[semi_conductive_screen]] layers, a [[metallic_sheath]], and an [[outer_protective_jacket]]. The design must account for [[minimum_bending_radius]], [[maximum_pulling_tension]], and [[installation_temperature]] constraints.

## Testing and Standards

After installation, cables must undergo [[after_installation_testing]] including [[high_voltage_withstand_test]], [[partial_discharge_measurement]], [[conductor_resistance_check]], [[dc_vlf_withstand_testing]], [[sheath_integrity_test]], and [[joint_resistance_measurement]]. Standards such as [[iec_61936_1]] and [[iec_62067]] govern the installation and testing procedures.

## Safety

Installation must comply with [[local_electrical_safety_regulations]] and [[company_specific_safety_procedures]], including [[cable_isolation_and_earthing]], [[voltage_absence_verification]], [[safety_locks_and_tags]], and establishment of a [[safe_work_zone_with_barriers]]. In case of faults, a [[fault_exclusion_zone]] and [[cable_fault_emergency_procedure]] must be followed.
