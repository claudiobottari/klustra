## Overview

**Super-clean XLPE compound** is a specialized grade of cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) insulation material designed for high-voltage cable systems operating at 66 kV and above. It is the dominant insulation choice for such systems due to its superior dielectric properties and long-term reliability.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:1]

## Properties

Super-clean XLPE compound exhibits the following key electrical and thermal characteristics:

- **Dielectric strength**: 20–30 kV/mm^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:2]
- **Continuous operating temperature**: up to 90 °C^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:2]
- **Emergency rating**: 130 °C for short duration^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:2]
- **Dielectric loss**: low (tan δ < 0.001)^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:2]

These properties enable the compound to meet the demanding requirements of [[high-voltage.cable.systems]].

## Manufacturing Process

The insulation is produced by [[peroxide.crosslinking]] during extrusion. A critical step is the [[triple.extrusion.process]], which applies the conductor screen, insulation, and insulation screen in a single pass to eliminate interfaces that could become weak points.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:3]

## Degradation Mechanisms and Longevity

Primary aging mechanisms for XLPE insulation are [[water.treeing]] and [[electrical.treeing]]. Modern super-clean XLPE compounds incorporate an [[antioxidant.package]] to mitigate these effects, achieving design lifetimes exceeding 40 years.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:4]
