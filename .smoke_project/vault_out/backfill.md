**Backfill** is the material used to refill a trench after a cable has been installed, and the process of placing and compacting that material. In [[high_voltage_cable_systems]], backfill is a critical step in [[direct_burial]] installations, providing mechanical protection, thermal stability, and long-term support for the cable.

## Requirements and procedure

For direct-buried cables, the trench is excavated to a minimum depth of 800 mm in [[underground_urban_networks]] and 1200 mm in rural areas. A [[sand_bedding]] layer of at least 100 mm is placed below and above the cable. [[warning_tape]] is installed 300 mm above the cable. The trench is then backfilled with compacted excavated material.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:1]

Backfill material must be free of sharp stones, debris, or large clods that could damage the cable’s [[outer_protective_jacket]]. In some specifications, the backfill is required to have a controlled thermal resistivity (typically ≤ 1.2 K·m/W) to avoid overheating of the cable core.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:1]

## Material types

Common backfill materials include:
- **Excavated native soil** – screened and compacted in layers.
- **Imported granular fill** – e.g., crushed stone or gravel with a specified grading curve.
- **Thermally enhanced backfill** – mixtures containing materials such as [[borealis_visico_le4253]] or [[exxonmobil_hdpe_compound]] to improve heat dissipation.

## Quality control

During backfilling, compaction is typically performed in layers of 200–300 mm to achieve a minimum of 90 % Proctor density. After compaction, the trench surface is restored to match the surrounding ground.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:1]
