## Overview

**Warning tape** is a safety device installed above buried cables to alert future excavators of the presence of underground utilities. It is typically a brightly colored (e.g., red, yellow, or orange) plastic or non‑woven fabric tape printed with a warning message such as “Caution: Buried Cable Below.” Its primary purpose is to reduce the risk of mechanical damage to cables during excavation, thereby preventing service outages, electrical hazards, and costly repairs.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Direct Burial]

## Installation practice

In direct‑burial installations, warning tape is placed **300 mm above the cable** (i.e., at a depth of about 500 mm in a typical 800 mm‑deep urban trench). The tape is laid continuously along the cable route, often with a slight overlap at joints. It is covered by backfill material, which is compacted to restore the ground surface.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Direct Burial]

## Standards and regulations

While the specific placement depth and tape type may be governed by [[local_electrical_safety_regulations]] or company‑specific procedures, the general requirement for a warning marker above buried cables is widely adopted in utility construction. For high‑voltage systems, additional measures such as [[fault_exclusion_zone]] marking may be required.^[4548a58ab1ab402c:Direct Burial]
