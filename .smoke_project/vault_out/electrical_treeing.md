## Overview

Electrical treeing is a primary aging mechanism in high-voltage cable insulation, particularly in cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) materials used for systems operating at 66 kV and above.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material] It involves the formation of tree-like, branched channels within the insulation due to partial discharges, which can propagate and eventually lead to dielectric breakdown.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]

## Mechanism

Electrical treeing occurs when localized electrical stress exceeds the dielectric strength of the insulation, initiating partial discharges that erode the material and create hollow channels.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material] These channels grow in a fractal, tree-like pattern, typically from defects or contaminants in the insulation.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material] The process is distinct from water treeing, another degradation mechanism, but both are considered primary aging mechanisms in XLPE cables.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]

## Impact on Cable Systems

Electrical treeing is a critical concern for high-voltage cable systems, as it can reduce the design lifetime of insulation.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material] Modern super-clean XLPE compounds, produced via peroxide crosslinking and triple extrusion, incorporate antioxidant packages to mitigate treeing and achieve design lifetimes exceeding 40 years.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material] However, electrical treeing remains a key failure mode, especially under high electrical stress or in the presence of impurities.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]

## Detection and Prevention

Detection of electrical treeing is typically performed through partial discharge measurement during routine or after-installation testing, as per standards like IEC 62067.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material] Prevention strategies include using super-clean XLPE compounds, maintaining proper manufacturing processes (e.g., triple extrusion to eliminate interfaces), and ensuring adequate design margins for dielectric strength (20-30 kV/mm) and operating temperature (up to 90°C continuous).^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]
