**Voltage absence verification** is a critical safety step performed before any work on high-voltage cable systems. It is part of the safe working practices mandated by [[iec.61936-1]], [[local.electrical.safety.regulations]], and [[company.specific.safety.procedures]].^[46d617e505500254:1]

## Procedure

The standard procedure requires:
1. Isolate and earth the cable at both ends.
2. Verify absence of voltage using approved indicators.
3. Apply [[safety.locks.and.tags]].
4. Establish a [[safe.work.zone.with.barriers]].^[46d617e505500254:2]

## Related safety measures

In case of a cable fault during live operation, an [[exclusion.zone.for.cable.fault]] of 3 m must be maintained around the suspected fault location, and [[fault.re-energization.prohibition]] applies until inspection. Immediate reporting to the [[control.centre.reporting]] is required.^[46d617e505500254:3]
