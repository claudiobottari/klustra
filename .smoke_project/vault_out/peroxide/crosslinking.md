Peroxide crosslinking is a chemical process employed in the production of cross-linked polyethylene ([[xlpe.insulation]]) insulation. During extrusion, peroxides create covalent crosslinks between polyethylene chains, transforming the thermoplastic into a thermoset with improved thermal and mechanical properties. This process is integral to manufacturing [[high-voltage.cable.systems]] insulation. The [[triple.extrusion.process]] applies conductor screen, insulation, and insulation screen in a single pass, with peroxide crosslinking occurring during this step ^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:Manufacturing Process]. Modern [[super.clean.xlpe.compound]] with [[antioxidant.package]] are used to achieve long design lifetimes ^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:Degradation Mechanisms].
