## Overview

Submarine crossings are high-voltage (HV) cable systems deployed underwater to transmit electrical power across bodies of water, such as seas, rivers, or lakes. They are a critical component of modern power transmission infrastructure, enabling connections between land masses, islands, and offshore installations. Modern submarine cables use cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) insulation for voltages up to 500 kV.^[8085135b0a96974f:1]

## Key Components

A submarine cable consists of the following layers, from the inside out:
- **Conductor**: Made of [[copper.conductor]] or [[aluminium.conductor]].^[8085135b0a96974f:2]
- **Semi-conductive screen**: A layer that ensures a uniform electric field.^[8085135b0a96974f:2]
- **XLPE insulation**: The primary dielectric layer, typically produced via a [[triple.extrusion.process]] and [[peroxide.crosslinking]].^[8085135b0a96974f:2]
- **Metallic sheath**: Provides water blocking and mechanical protection.^[8085135b0a96974f:2]
- **Outer protective jacket**: The outermost layer, often made of high-density polyethylene (HDPE) for abrasion and moisture resistance.^[8085135b0a96974f:2]

## Applications

Submarine crossings are used in several key applications:
- **[[submarine.crossings]]**: Direct connections between land masses or islands.
- **[[offshore.wind.farm.connections]]**: Transmitting power from offshore wind turbines to onshore grids.^[8085135b0a96974f:3]
- **[[underground.urban.networks]]**: In some cases, submarine cables are used for river or harbor crossings within urban areas.^[8085135b0a96974f:3]

## Installation Considerations

Installation of submarine cables requires careful planning and specialized equipment. Key factors include:
- **[[bending.radii]]**: Cables must be handled within specified [[installation.minimum.bending.radius]] to avoid damage.^[8085135b0a96974f:3]
- **[[installation.maximum.pulling.tension]]**: Tension during laying must be controlled to prevent conductor or sheath damage.
- **[[installation.temperature]]**: Ambient and cable temperature affect handling and performance.
- **[[installation.direct.burial]]**: Cables are often buried in the seabed for protection, using techniques such as trenching or jetting.
- **[[installation.trench.depth]]**: Burial depth depends on water depth, seabed conditions, and risk of anchor damage.
- **[[installation.sand.bedding]]**: A sand or gravel layer may be used to cushion the cable.
- **[[installation.warning.tape]]**: Warning tapes or markers are placed above buried cables to alert future seabed activities.

## Testing and Quality Assurance

Submarine cables undergo rigorous testing to ensure reliability:
- **[[cable.testing.type_testing]]**: Performed on prototype cables to verify design.
- **[[cable.testing.routine_testing]]**: Conducted on each manufactured length.
- **[[cable.testing.after_installation_testing]]**: Includes [[cable.testing.high_voltage_withstand_test]], [[cable.testing.partial_discharge_measurement]], and [[cable.testing.sheath_integrity_test]].
- **[[cable.testing.dc_vlf_withstand_testing]]**: Often used for field testing of long submarine cables.

## Safety and Regulations

Installation and maintenance must comply with:
- **[[iec.61936-1]]**: Standard for power installations exceeding 1 kV AC.
- **[[local.electrical.safety.regulations]]**: National and regional codes.
- **[[company.specific.safety.procedures]]**: Including [[cable.isolation.and.earthing]], [[voltage.absence.verification]], and [[safety.locks.and.tags]].
- **[[fault.re-energization.prohibition]]**: Strict protocols to prevent re-energization during fault conditions.
