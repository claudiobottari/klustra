## Overview

**Cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) insulation** is a thermoset polymer used as the primary electrical insulation in modern high-voltage cable systems. It is formed by cross-linking polyethylene molecules, typically via a peroxide-initiated chemical reaction, which transforms the thermoplastic into a durable, heat-resistant material. XLPE insulation enables cable operation at voltages up to 500 kV and is the dominant insulation type for underground, submarine, and offshore wind farm power transmission cables.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:1]

## Material properties

XLPE offers a combination of high [[dielectric_strength]], low [[dielectric_loss]], and a high continuous [[operating_temperature]] (typically 90 °C for normal operation, with emergency ratings up to 130 °C). The cross-linked structure provides excellent resistance to thermal deformation and mechanical stress, contributing to a [[design_lifetime]] of 30–40 years under rated conditions.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:1]

## Manufacturing process

The insulation is applied to the conductor using a [[triple_extrusion_process]], where the inner [[semi_conductive_screen]], the XLPE insulation layer, and the outer semi-conductive screen are extruded simultaneously. Cross-linking is achieved by heating the extruded cable core in a continuous vulcanisation (CV) tube, initiating [[peroxide_crosslinking]] of the polyethylene chains.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:1]

## Degradation mechanisms

Two primary ageing mechanisms affect XLPE insulation: [[water_treeing]] and [[electrical_treeing]]. Water treeing occurs in the presence of moisture and electric stress, while electrical treeing is a pre-breakdown phenomenon initiated by defects or contaminants. Modern [[super_clean_xlpe_compounds]] and dry-cure processes have significantly reduced susceptibility to these mechanisms.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:1]

## Applications

XLPE-insulated cables are used in [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] for [[underground_urban_networks]], [[submarine_crossings]], and connections to [[offshore_wind_farms]]. The insulation’s low dielectric loss and high thermal rating make it suitable for high-power-density routes where space is constrained.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:1]

## Installation considerations

During installation, the cable must respect a [[minimum_bending_radius]] (typically 15–20 times the cable diameter) and a [[maximum_pulling_tension]] to avoid damaging the XLPE insulation. [[installation_temperature]] limits (usually ≥ 0 °C) prevent cracking of the insulation during bending.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:1]

## Testing

XLPE-insulated cables are subjected to [[type_testing]], [[routine_testing]], and [[after_installation_testing]] per standards such as [[iec_62067]]. Tests include [[high_voltage_withstand_test]], [[partial_discharge_measurement]], [[conductor_resistance_check]], [[dc_vlf_withstand_testing]], [[sheath_integrity_test]], and [[joint_resistance_measurement]].^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:1]
