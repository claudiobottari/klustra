**IEC 62067** is the international standard governing power cables with extruded insulation and their accessories for rated voltages above 150 kV (Um = 170 kV) up to 500 kV (Um = 550 kV). It defines comprehensive testing, design, and installation requirements to ensure reliability and safety of [[high_voltage_cable_systems]].

## Testing Procedures

IEC 62067 specifies three categories of tests:

### Type Testing
Type tests validate the cable system design and include:
- **Electrical tests**: withstand voltage, partial discharge measurement, and tan delta measurement
- **Mechanical tests**: bending test and tensile test
- **Thermal tests**: load cycling at rated and emergency temperature

These tests ensure the cable system meets the required performance under extreme conditions.^[362871cd3841da0e:section:Cable Testing Standards]

### Routine Testing
Every manufactured cable length must undergo routine tests:
1. High-voltage withstand test at 2.5 U0 for 30 minutes
2. Partial discharge measurement (threshold < 5 pC)
3. Conductor resistance check

These tests verify the integrity of each production unit.^[362871cd3841da0e:section:Cable Testing Standards]

### After-Installation Testing
On-site commissioning tests include:
- DC or VLF withstand testing
- Sheath integrity test (10 kV DC for 1 minute)
- Joint resistance measurement

These tests confirm that the installed system is free from damage and correctly assembled.^[362871cd3841da0e:section:Cable Testing Standards]
