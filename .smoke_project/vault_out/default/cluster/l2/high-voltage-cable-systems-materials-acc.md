The reliability and performance of high-voltage cable systems depend critically on both the quality of the insulation materials used in the cable itself and the integrity of the accessories that connect and terminate those cables. This cluster brings together two foundational aspects of high-voltage cable engineering: the approved crosslinkable XLPE compounds that form the primary insulation layer, and the pre-moulded and cold-shrink joints and terminations that ensure system continuity.

[[default.cluster.l1.hv-cable-xlpe-insulation-compounds]] focuses on the specific crosslinkable polyethylene (XLPE) compounds approved for use in high-voltage cable manufacturing. These materials must meet stringent electrical and thermal performance requirements to withstand the high electric field stresses and operating temperatures typical of transmission and distribution networks. The cluster documents approved suppliers and their specific product lines, ensuring that only qualified compounds are used in cable production.

[[default.cluster.l1.high-voltage-cable-accessories-suppliers]] addresses the complementary challenge of connecting and terminating these cables. It covers approved suppliers and product lines for pre-moulded and cold-shrink joints and terminations, which must be compatible with the XLPE-insulated cables and comply with IEC 62067 standards. Together, these two clusters provide a complete picture of the material and component ecosystem required for building reliable high-voltage cable systems.
