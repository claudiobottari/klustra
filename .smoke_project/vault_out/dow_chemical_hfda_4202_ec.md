'''Dow Chemical HFDA-4202 EC''' is an extra-clean cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) compound approved for use as insulation material in high-voltage cable systems.

== Approved use ==
The compound is designated for application in [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] and is categorised as an insulation compound, specifically as an extra-clean, peroxide-crosslinkable XLPE material.^[f55e45b580b53352:5]

It appears on an approved supplier list for high voltage cable components, alongside the Borealis Visico LE4253 product which serves a similar purpose.^[f55e45b580b53352:5]

The Dow Chemical HFDA-4202 EC compound is intended for use with conductor materials such as aluminium rod from [[Norsk_Hydro]] and copper rod from [[Aurubis_AG]], and with sheathing materials including [[ExxonMobil_HDPE_compound]] and [[Arkema_aluminium_pe_laminate_tape]].^[f55e45b580b53352:2-5]

== Properties ==
As an XLPE insulation material, Dow Chemical HFDA-4202 EC exhibits properties including [[dielectric_strength]], defined [[operating_temperature]] limits, and low [[dielectric_loss]].^[f55e45b580b53352:5] The extra-clean designation indicates that the compound is formulated to reduce the occurrence of contaminants that can lead to [[electrical_treeing]] or [[water_treeing]] in service.^[f55e45b580b53352:5]
