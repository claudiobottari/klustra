## hv_cable_installation_pulling_tension

**Pulling tension** is the axial force applied to a [[high_voltage_cable_systems|high‑voltage cable]] during its installation, primarily when pulling the cable into ducts or trenches. Excessive tension can damage the conductor, insulation, or sheath, so limits are strictly defined.

### Maximum pulling tension

During installation, the **maximum pulling tension** must not exceed **50 N/mm²** on the conductor cross‑section.^[8913d160a82c281a:2] This limit is applied to the conductor (the central load‑bearing element) to prevent permanent elongation or breakage.

### Monitoring and control

Pulling tension is monitored using a **cable winch with tension monitoring** equipment.^[8913d160a82c281a:5] Operators must watch the tension gauge continuously and stop the pull if the limit is approached. The installation rate is typically **5–10 m/min** for duct pulls, which helps keep tension steady.^[8913d160a82c281a:5]

### Related installation parameters

- [[hv_cable_installation_bending_radius|Minimum bending radius]]: 20× cable outer diameter.^[8913d160a82c281a:2]
- [[hv_cable_installation_temperature_conditions|Minimum installation temperature]]: 0 °C.^[8913d160a82c281a:2]
- [[hv_cable_duct_installation_process|Duct installation]] requires clean, lubricated ducts and a compatible lubricant.^[8913d160a82c281a:4–5]
- [[hv_cable_direct_burial_installation|Direct burial]] has its own trench depth and bedding requirements, but pulling tension limits apply to the cable itself regardless of installation method.^[8913d160a82c281a:7–8]
