## Properties

XLPE insulation exhibits a dielectric strength of 20–30 kV/mm, low dielectric loss (tan δ < 0.001), and a continuous operating temperature of up to 90 °C, with an emergency rating of 130 °C for short durations.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:properties] These characteristics make it suitable for [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] operating at 66 kV and above.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:xlpe_dominant]

## Manufacturing Process

XLPE insulation is produced by peroxide crosslinking during extrusion. The triple-extrusion process applies the conductor screen, insulation layer, and insulation screen in a single pass, eliminating interfaces that could trap contaminants.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:manufacturing] The resulting [[xlpe_manufacturing_process]] yields a homogeneous dielectric structure.

## Degradation Mechanisms

Water treeing and electrical treeing are the primary aging mechanisms. Modern super-clean XLPE compounds, enhanced with antioxidant packages, achieve design lifetimes exceeding 40 years.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:degradation] Ongoing research into [[xlpe_degradation_mechanisms]] continues to improve long-term reliability.

## Role in Cable Systems

In [[high_voltage_cable_systems]], the cable core consists of a copper or aluminium conductor surrounded by a semi-conductive screen, the XLPE insulation layer, a metallic sheath for water blocking, and an outer protective jacket.^[8085135b0a96974f:key_components] XLPE insulation is used for voltages up to 500 kV.^[8085135b0a96974f:xlpe_voltage] The material’s performance is critical to [[hv_cable_applications]] such as underground urban networks, submarine crossings, and connections to offshore wind farms.^[8085135b0a96974f:applications]
