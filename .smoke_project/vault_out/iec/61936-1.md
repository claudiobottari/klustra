## Overview

**IEC 61936-1** is an international standard titled *Power installations exceeding 1 kV a.c.* It establishes safety requirements for the design, erection, and operation of high-voltage (HV) electrical installations, including [[high-voltage.cable.systems]]. The standard is a key reference for ensuring safe working practices on cables, joints, terminations, and associated equipment.

## Scope and Application

IEC 61936-1 applies to all power installations with nominal voltages above 1 kV alternating current. It covers:
- Design and construction of HV cable systems
- Selection of components such as [[copper.conductor]] or [[aluminium.conductor]], [[xlpe.insulation]], [[semi-conductive.screen]], [[metallic.sheath]], and [[outer.protective.jacket]]
- Installation methods including [[installation.direct.burial]], [[installation.duct.installation]], and [[installation.jointing]]
- Testing procedures referenced in [[iec.62067]] and [[cable.testing.type_testing]]

## Safety Requirements

All HV cable work must comply with IEC 61936-1 alongside [[local.electrical.safety.regulations]] and [[company.specific.safety.procedures]]^[46d617e505500254:1].

### Safe Working Practices

Before any work on cable systems, the following steps are mandatory:
1. [[cable.isolation.and.earthing]] – isolate and earth the cable at both ends
2. [[voltage.absence.verification]] – verify absence of voltage using approved indicators
3. [[safety.locks.and.tags]] – apply safety locks and tags
4. [[safe.work.zone.with.barriers]] – establish a safe work zone with barriers^[46d617e505500254:2]

### Emergency Procedures

In case of a cable fault during live operation:
- Maintain an [[exclusion.zone.for.cable.fault]] of 3 m around the suspected fault location
- [[fault.re-energization.prohibition]] – do not attempt to re-energize without inspection
- [[control.centre.reporting]] – report immediately to the control centre^[46d617e505500254:3]

## Testing and Commissioning

After installation, cable systems must undergo testing as per [[cable.testing.after_installation_testing]], including:
- [[cable.testing.high_voltage_withstand_test]]
- [[cable.testing.partial_discharge_measurement]]
- [[cable.testing.conductor_resistance_check]]
- [[cable.testing.dc_vlf_withstand_testing]]
- [[cable.testing.sheath_integrity_test]]
- [[cable.testing.joint_resistance_measurement]]

These tests help detect defects such as [[water.treeing]] and [[electrical.treeing]], which are mitigated by using [[super.clean.xlpe.compound]] with an [[antioxidant.package]] produced via [[triple.extrusion.process]] and [[peroxide.crosslinking]].
