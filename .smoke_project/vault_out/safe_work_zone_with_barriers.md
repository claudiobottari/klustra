## Safe Work Zone with Barriers

A **safe work zone with barriers** is a physically demarcated area established around high-voltage (HV) cable work sites to protect personnel from electrical hazards, moving equipment, and accidental contact with energized components. It is a mandatory element of safe working practices for HV cable systems.

### Purpose

The primary purpose is to prevent unauthorized or inadvertent entry into a hazardous area where cable isolation, jointing, testing, or repair is in progress. The zone also serves to contain tools, materials, and debris, reducing trip hazards and ensuring a clear escape route in an emergency.

### Establishment

Before any work on cable systems, the following steps must be completed:
1. Isolate and earth the cable at both ends.
2. Verify absence of voltage using approved indicators.
3. Apply safety locks and tags.
4. Establish safe work zone with barriers.^[b359cf61b264db52:1]

Barriers may include:
- Cones, tapes, or portable fence panels
- Warning signs (e.g., "DANGER – HIGH VOLTAGE – KEEP OUT")
- Lockable gates if the zone is in a public or shared area

### Regulatory Basis

All HV cable work must comply with:
- IEC 61936‑1 (Power installations exceeding 1 kV)
- Local electrical safety regulations
- Company-specific safety procedures^[b359cf61b264db52:1]

### Relationship to Other Safety Measures

The safe work zone with barriers is part of a layered safety approach that also includes:
- [[cable_isolation_and_earthing]]
- [[voltage_absence_verification]]
- [[safety_locks_and_tags]]
- [[fault_exclusion_zone]] (for live fault emergencies)

### Emergency Considerations

In the event of a cable fault during live operation, a separate [[fault_exclusion_zone]] of 3 m around the suspected fault location is maintained, and no attempt to re‑energize is made without inspection.^[b359cf61b264db52:1] The safe work zone with barriers is distinct from this emergency exclusion zone.
