## Overview

Safety locks and tags are a mandatory step in the safe working procedure for high-voltage cable work. They are applied after the cable has been isolated, earthed, and verified to be de-energized. This measure ensures that the cable remains isolated and cannot be accidentally re-energized while work is in progress. The procedure is required by IEC 61936-1 and local electrical safety regulations. ^[46d617e505500254:Safety Standards for HV Cable Work]

## Procedure

According to the standard procedure:
1. Isolate and earth the cable at both ends.
2. Verify absence of voltage using approved indicators.
3. Apply safety locks and tags.
4. Establish safe work zone with barriers. ^[46d617e505500254:Safety Standards for HV Cable Work]

These steps must be followed before any work on cable systems. ^[46d617e505500254:Safety Standards for HV Cable Work]
