## Overview

A **cable winch** is an essential tool for pulling [[high-voltage.cable.systems]] through ducts or trenches during installation. It is designed to apply controlled tension to the cable, preventing damage to the conductor, insulation, or sheath. The winch is typically used in conjunction with [[installation.cable.lubricant]] and a [[installation.duct.installation]] setup.

## Operational Parameters

To ensure safe and effective cable pulling, the winch must operate within the following constraints:

- **Maximum pulling tension**: The tension applied to the conductor must not exceed 50 N/mm².^[8913d160a82c281a]
- **Minimum bending radius**: The cable must not be bent to a radius smaller than 20 times its outer diameter during pulling.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- **Installation temperature**: The ambient and cable temperature must be above 0 °C to avoid sheath cracking or excessive stiffness.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- **Pulling speed**: A steady rate of 5–10 m/min is recommended to maintain control and avoid sudden jerks.^[8913d160a82c281a]

## Tension Monitoring

A critical feature of the cable winch is **tension monitoring**, which provides real-time feedback to the operator. This allows immediate adjustment if the pulling force approaches the [[installation.maximum.pulling.tension]] limit, thereby protecting the cable from overstress.^[8913d160a82c281a]

## Installation Practices

- The winch is typically positioned at the pulling end of the duct or trench, with the cable reel placed at the opposite end.
- A compatible [[installation.cable.lubricant]] is applied to reduce friction and prevent sheath damage.
- The winch operator must coordinate with personnel at the cable feed end to ensure smooth, continuous pulling.
- After installation, the cable should be inspected for any signs of damage, and the pulling tension record should be reviewed.
