## installation.sand.bedding

Sand bedding is a layer of sand used in [[installation.direct.burial]] installations to provide a uniform, protective foundation for high-voltage cables. The sand cushions the cable against sharp objects in the soil, reduces mechanical stress during backfilling, and improves drainage around the cable. 

**Specifications:**
- A sand bedding layer of **100 mm** must be placed **below** the cable.  
- A further **100 mm** sand layer is placed **above** the cable before the trench is backfilled with excavated material.  

These requirements are part of the standard direct‑burial procedure, which also specifies a minimum [[installation.trench.depth]] (800 mm in urban areas, 1200 mm in rural areas) and the placement of [[installation.warning.tape]] 300 mm above the cable.^[8913d160a82c281a]

The sand used should be clean, free of stones and organic matter, and have a particle size that ensures good compaction without damaging the cable sheath.
