**Installation warning tape** is a brightly colored, durable tape placed in the ground above buried [[high-voltage.cable.systems]] to alert future excavators of the presence of underground cables. It serves as a visual barrier and a last line of defense against accidental dig-ins, which can cause service outages, safety hazards, and costly repairs.

## Purpose and function
Warning tape is not a structural or electrical component; its sole purpose is to provide a visible and physical warning during any subsequent excavation work. It is typically laid at a specified depth above the cable, so that any digging equipment will encounter the tape before reaching the cable itself. The tape often bears a printed message such as "CAUTION: BURIED ELECTRIC CABLE BELOW" or similar wording required by [[local.electrical.safety.regulations]].

## Installation requirements
According to standard installation practices for direct burial of high-voltage cables, warning tape must be placed **300 mm above the cable** after the sand bedding and initial backfill have been completed. The tape is laid continuously along the cable route, with overlaps at joints to ensure no gaps. The tape should be of a color that contrasts with the surrounding soil (commonly bright yellow or red) and made of a non-corrodible, rot-proof material such as polyethylene.

### Depth and positioning
- The tape is positioned **300 mm above the cable** in the trench.^[8913d160a82c281a:direct_burial]
- This depth ensures that any mechanical excavator will strike the tape before reaching the cable, giving the operator a final warning.
- In multi-cable trenches, a separate warning tape may be required for each cable or for the entire group, depending on [[company.specific.safety.procedures]].

### Material and durability
- The tape must be resistant to soil chemicals, moisture, and UV degradation (if exposed during installation).
- It should be detectable by metal detectors if it includes a metallic foil or wire, though this is not always required.
- The tape must remain legible for the design life of the cable installation.

## Related safety measures
Warning tape is part of a broader set of safety measures for buried cables, which also include:
- [[installation.sand.bedding]] (100 mm below and above the cable)
- [[installation.trench.depth]] (minimum 800 mm urban, 1200 mm rural)
- [[safe.work.zone.with.barriers]] and [[exclusion.zone.for.cable.fault]] during live work
- [[cable.isolation.and.earthing]] and [[voltage.absence.verification]] before any excavation near known cables

## Standards and regulations
The use and positioning of warning tape are typically governed by [[local.electrical.safety.regulations]] and may be referenced in [[iec.61936-1]] or national codes. Installers must follow [[company.specific.safety.procedures]] to ensure compliance.
