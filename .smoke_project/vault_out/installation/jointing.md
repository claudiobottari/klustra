## General requirements

All joints must be installed in a controlled environment to protect the components from moisture, dust, and temperature extremes.^[8913d160a82c281a]

## Joint bay dimensions

Joint bays require minimum dimensions of 3 m × 1.5 m to provide adequate working space for the jointer and to accommodate the joint and its bending restrictions.^[8913d160a82c281a]

## Types of joints

Prefabricated joints are preferred for faster installation.^[8913d160a82c281a] These are factory-tested, ready-to-install units that reduce on-site variability and installation time.

## Environmental control

During jointing, the environment must be controlled (e.g., humidity, temperature, cleanliness) to prevent contamination that could lead to premature insulation failure.^[8913d160a82c281a]

## Preparation

Before jointing, the cable must be isolated, earthed, and verified as de-energized in accordance with local safety regulations.^[8913d160a82c281a] The cable ends should be cleaned and dried thoroughly.

## Testing after jointing

After installation, the joint must be tested to ensure integrity. The guide does not specify particular tests, but standard practice includes high voltage withstand and partial discharge measurement.^[8913d160a82c281a]
