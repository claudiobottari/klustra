## Overview

**Direct burial** is a cable installation method where the cable is laid directly into the ground, without the use of a protective duct. It is commonly employed for [[high-voltage.cable.systems]] in [[underground.urban.networks]], [[submarine.crossings]], and [[offshore.wind.farm.connections]] when the cable is designed for such exposure. The procedure requires careful trench preparation, controlled pulling, and protection against mechanical damage and environmental factors.

## General Requirements

These requirements apply to all direct-burial installations:

- **Minimum bending radius during installation**: 20× the cable outer diameter.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- **Maximum pulling tension**: Must not exceed 50 N/mm² on the conductor.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- **Installation temperature**: Must be above 0 °C.^[8913d160a82c281a]

## Trenching and Bedding

- **Trench depth**: Minimum 800 mm in urban areas and 1200 mm in rural areas.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- **Sand bedding**: A layer of sand 100 mm thick shall be placed both below and above the cable.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- **Warning tape**: A warning tape is placed 300 mm above the cable.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- **Backfill**: The trench is backfilled with compacted excavated material.^[8913d160a82c281a]

## Cable Pulling

Direct-burial cables are pulled directly into the trench using a cable winch. The following practices are recommended:

- Apply a [[installation.cable.lubricant]] compatible with the outer sheath material.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- Use a [[installation.cable.winch]] with tension monitoring.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- Maintain a steady pulling rate of 5–10 m/min.^[8913d160a82c281a]

## Jointing

Joints on direct-buried cables must be installed in a controlled environment. Prefabricated joints are preferred for faster installation. Joint bays require minimum dimensions of 3 m × 1.5 m.^[8913d160a82c281a]

## Alternative Methods

For comparison, the same source also describes [[installation.duct.installation]], where the cable is pulled into a pre-installed duct. The general requirements for bending radius, pulling tension, and temperature apply to both methods.^[8913d160a82c281a]
