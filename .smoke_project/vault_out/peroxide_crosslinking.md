## Overview

Peroxide crosslinking is the dominant method for producing cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) insulation in high-voltage cable systems operating at 66 kV and above. The process involves the use of organic peroxides, such as dicumyl peroxide, which decompose at elevated temperatures to generate free radicals. These radicals initiate a chemical reaction that forms covalent crosslinks between polyethylene polymer chains, transforming the thermoplastic material into a thermoset elastomer with superior properties.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]

## Process Details

Peroxide crosslinking is integrated into the cable manufacturing process, most notably during the triple-extrusion process. In this method, the conductor screen, insulation, and insulation screen are applied in a single pass to eliminate interfaces and ensure uniform crosslinking. The extrusion is followed by a curing stage where the cable core is heated to activate the peroxide, typically in a continuous vulcanization (CV) tube using steam or nitrogen under pressure.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]

## Properties of Crosslinked Material

The crosslinked structure imparts several key properties to XLPE insulation:
- **Dielectric strength**: 20–30 kV/mm^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]
- **Operating temperature**: Up to 90°C continuous, with emergency ratings of 130°C for short durations^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]
- **Low dielectric loss**: tan δ < 0.001^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]
- **Design lifetime**: Exceeding 40 years with modern super-clean XLPE compounds and antioxidant packages^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]

## Degradation Mechanisms

Despite its robustness, XLPE insulation is subject to aging mechanisms, primarily water treeing and electrical treeing. These are mitigated through the use of super-clean XLPE compounds and advanced antioxidant packages, which help achieve the long design lifetimes required for high-voltage applications.^[57e907ec63549ebf:XLPE Insulation Material]
