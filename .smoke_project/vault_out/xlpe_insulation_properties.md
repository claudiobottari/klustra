Cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) is the dominant insulation material for [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] operating at 66 kV and above.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:1]

## Electrical Properties

XLPE exhibits a dielectric strength of 20–30 kV/mm and low dielectric loss (tan δ < 0.001).^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:4]

## Thermal Properties

The material can operate continuously at temperatures up to 90°C, with an emergency rating of 130°C for short durations.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:4]

## Manufacturing Context

XLPE insulation is produced by peroxide crosslinking during extrusion. The triple-extrusion process applies conductor screen, insulation, and insulation screen in a single pass to eliminate interfaces.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:6]

## Degradation and Lifetime

Water treeing and electrical treeing are the primary aging mechanisms. Modern super-clean XLPE compounds with antioxidant packages achieve design lifetimes exceeding 40 years.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:8]
