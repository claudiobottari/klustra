The **HV cable duct installation process** is a method of installing [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] within protective conduits (ducts) to provide mechanical protection and facilitate future cable replacement. This process is governed by specific requirements for bending radius, pulling tension, temperature, and lubrication.

## General Requirements
Before any duct installation, the following general conditions must be met:
- **Minimum bending radius**: 20× the cable outer diameter during installation.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- **Maximum pulling tension**: 50 N/mm² on the conductor.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- **Installation temperature**: Must be above 0 °C.^[8913d160a82c281a]

## Duct Preparation
- All ducts must be cleaned and inspected before pulling the cable to remove debris and ensure a smooth path.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- A cable lubricant compatible with the outer sheath material must be applied to reduce friction.^[8913d160a82c281a]

## Pulling Procedure
- A cable winch with tension monitoring is required to control and record pulling forces.^[8913d160a82c281a]
- The cable should be installed at a steady rate of 5–10 m/min to avoid sudden jerks or damage.^[8913d160a82c281a]

## Post-Installation
After pulling, the cable must be inspected for sheath integrity and any signs of damage. Jointing, if required, must follow [[hv_cable_jointing_requirements]] and be performed in a controlled environment.^[8913d160a82c281a] Prefabricated joints are preferred for faster installation, and joint bays require minimum dimensions of 3 m × 1.5 m.^[8913d160a82c281a]
