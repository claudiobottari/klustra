## Purpose

A safe work zone with barriers prevents unauthorised entry and provides a clear visual boundary around the cable or equipment being worked on. It is a critical element of the safe system of work required by [[iec.61936-1]], [[local.electrical.safety.regulations]], and [[company.specific.safety.procedures]].

## Procedure

After completing the preceding safety steps – [[cable.isolation.and.earthing]], [[voltage.absence.verification]], and [[safety.locks.and.tags]] – the work team must:

1. Erect physical barriers (e.g., cones, tape, fencing) around the work area.
2. Post warning signs indicating high voltage and restricted access.
3. Ensure the zone is large enough to accommodate the cable, joints, and any necessary tools without encroaching on adjacent live equipment.

The establishment of the safe work zone with barriers is explicitly listed as the fourth step in safe working practices for HV cable work.^[46d617e505500254:1]

## Relationship to other safety measures

- The safe work zone complements the [[exclusion.zone.for.cable.fault]] (3 m radius around a suspected fault location during live operation).
- It is distinct from the prohibition on [[fault.re-energization.prohibition]] and the requirement to [[control.centre.reporting|report to the control centre]] in emergency situations.

## Compliance

All personnel must be trained in the correct setup of barriers and the meaning of the demarcated zone. Regular audits verify that barriers are in place before work commences.
