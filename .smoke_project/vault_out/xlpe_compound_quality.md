## XLPE Compound Quality

XLPE (cross-linked polyethylene) compound quality is a critical factor in the performance and longevity of [[high_voltage_cable_systems]] operating at 66 kV and above. The compound must meet stringent purity and processing standards to achieve the required [[xlpe_insulation_properties]].

### Key Properties
- **Dielectric strength**: 20–30 kV/mm^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:XLPE Insulation Material]
- **Continuous operating temperature**: up to 90 °C^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:XLPE Insulation Material]
- **Emergency rating**: 130 °C for short duration^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:XLPE Insulation Material]
- **Dielectric loss (tan δ)**: < 0.001^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:XLPE Insulation Material]

### Manufacturing Process
XLPE insulation is produced by peroxide crosslinking during extrusion. The triple-extrusion process applies [[cable_core_components|conductor screen, insulation, and insulation screen]] in a single pass to eliminate interfaces, which is essential for maintaining compound purity.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:XLPE Insulation Material]

### Degradation Mechanisms
Water treeing and electrical treeing are the primary aging mechanisms. Modern super-clean XLPE compounds with antioxidant packages achieve design lifetimes exceeding 40 years.^[392adbd3c4ad7a29:XLPE Insulation Material]

### Quality Assurance
Compound quality is verified through [[hv_cable_type_testing|type testing]] and [[hv_cable_routine_testing|routine testing]] as per [[iec_62067]]. Key tests include [[partial_discharge_measurement]], [[high_voltage_withstand_test]], [[sheath_integrity_test]], and [[joint_resistance_measurement]]. Approved suppliers for insulation compounds include [[borealis_visico_le4253_xlpe|Borealis Visico LE4253]] and [[dow_hfda_4202_ec_xlpe|Dow HFDA‑4202 EC]].
