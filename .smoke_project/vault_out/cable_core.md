## Definition and role

The **cable core** is the innermost functional unit of a [[high_voltage_cable_systems|high‑voltage cable system]]. It consists of a metallic conductor (either [[copper]] or [[aluminium]]) that carries the current, surrounded by a sequence of insulating and protective layers. The core is responsible for conducting electrical power while maintaining electrical integrity and mechanical robustness during installation and service.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

## Components

From the conductor outward, the cable core comprises:^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

1. **Conductor** – A stranded or solid copper or aluminium wire that provides the low‑resistance path for current.
2. **[[semi_conductive_screen]]** – A thin layer of semi‑conductive material applied directly over the conductor to smooth the electric field and prevent partial discharges.
3. **[[cross_linked_polyethylene_insulation|XLPE insulation layer]]** – The main dielectric, typically cross‑linked polyethylene, which provides high [[dielectric_strength]] and allows operation at elevated [[operating_temperature|temperatures]].
4. **[[metallic_sheath]]** – A metallic layer (e.g., lead, aluminium, or copper) that acts as a water‑blocking barrier and can also serve as a return path for fault currents.
5. **[[outer_protective_jacket]]** – An extruded polymer jacket that protects the core from mechanical damage, moisture, and environmental attack.

## Context in cable systems

The cable core is manufactured as a continuous assembly using processes such as [[triple_extrusion_process|triple extrusion]] for XLPE cables. Its design must satisfy [[minimum_bending_radius|bending radius]] and [[maximum_pulling_tension|pulling tension]] limits to avoid damage during installation. The core is the part of the cable that is [[jointing|jointed]] or terminated; prefabricated accessories (e.g., [[prysmian_pre_moulded_joints_and_terminations|pre‑moulded joints]]) are designed to match the core geometry.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

## Testing

After installation, the integrity of the cable core is verified through [[after_installation_testing|post‑installation tests]] such as [[high_voltage_withstand_test|high‑voltage withstand tests]], [[partial_discharge_measurement|partial discharge measurement]], and [[conductor_resistance_check|conductor resistance checks]]. These tests ensure that the core has not been damaged during handling and that its insulation is free of defects.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]
