## Overview

High-voltage (HV) cable systems are critical infrastructure for power transmission, typically using cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) insulation for voltages up to 500 kV.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

## Key Components

The cable core consists of a copper or aluminium conductor surrounded by:
- Semi-conductive screen
- XLPE insulation layer
- Metallic sheath for water blocking
- Outer protective jacket^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

These components are collectively referred to as the [[cable_core]]. The [[semi_conductive_screen]] ensures a uniform electric field, while the [[metallic_sheath]] provides water‑blocking and fault‑current return. The [[outer_protective_jacket]] protects against mechanical damage and environmental exposure.

## Applications

HV cables are deployed in [[underground_urban_networks]], [[submarine_crossings]], and connections to [[offshore_wind_farms]]. Installation requires careful handling of [[minimum_bending_radius]] and thermal considerations.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

## Installation Practices

### Mechanical Limits
- **Minimum bending radius**: Must be respected to avoid damaging the [[cable_core]] and insulation.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]
- **Maximum pulling tension**: Controlled during [[duct_installation]] using [[cable_winch_tension_monitoring]].^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]
- **Installation temperature**: Ambient conditions affect cable flexibility and lubricant performance.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

### Methods
- **[[duct_installation]]**: Cable is pulled into pre‑laid ducts, often with [[cable_lubricant]] to reduce friction.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]
- **[[direct_burial]]**: Cable is laid in a trench at specified [[trench_depth]], on [[sand_bedding]], covered with [[backfill]], and protected by [[warning_tape]].^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

### Jointing
- [[jointing]] uses [[prefabricated_joints]] or field‑applied joints; [[joint_bay_dimensions]] must accommodate the joint housing and allow for testing.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]
- Common joint types include [[prysmian_pre_moulded_joints_and_terminations]] and [[nkt_cold_shrink_joints]].^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

## Safety Procedures

Before any work on HV cables, the following must be performed:
- [[cable_isolation_and_earthing]]
- [[voltage_absence_verification]]
- Application of [[safety_locks_and_tags]]
- Establishment of a [[safe_work_zone_with_barriers]]
- Definition of a [[fault_exclusion_zone]]
- Adherence to [[cable_fault_emergency_procedure]]^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

These procedures comply with [[iec_61936_1]], [[local_electrical_safety_regulations]], and [[company_specific_safety_procedures]].^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

## Materials

### Insulation
- [[xlpe_insulation_material]] is produced via [[peroxide_crosslinking]] and [[triple_extrusion_process]].^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]
- Key properties: high [[dielectric_strength]], low [[dielectric_loss]], and [[operating_temperature]] up to 90 °C (continuous).^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]
- Degradation mechanisms include [[water_treeing]] and [[electrical_treeing]]; mitigated by [[super_clean_xlpe_compounds]].^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]
- Common compounds: [[borealis_visico_le4253]], [[dow_chemical_hfda_4202_ec]], [[exxonmobil_hdpe_compound]].^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

### Sheath and Jacket
- [[arkema_aluminium_pe_laminate_tape]] is used for moisture barrier.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]

Specific tests:

## Design Lifetime

The [[design_lifetime]] of HV cable systems is typically 30–40 years under normal operating conditions.^[acd06c178fe5b8e6:narrative]
