## High-Voltage Cable Systems

**High-Voltage Cable Systems** (HV cable systems) are critical infrastructure for power transmission. Modern HV cables use [[xlpe_insulation|cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) insulation]] for voltages up to 500 kV.^[8085135b0a96974f:High-Voltage Cable Systems]

### Key Components

The cable core consists of a copper or aluminium conductor surrounded by semi-conductive screen, XLPE insulation layer, metallic sheath for water blocking, and an outer protective jacket. These components are collectively known as [[cable_core_components]].^[8085135b0a96974f:High-Voltage Cable Systems]

### Applications

HV cables are deployed in underground urban networks, submarine crossings, and connections to offshore wind farms. Installation requires careful handling of [[hv_cable_installation_bending_radius|bending radii]] and [[hv_cable_installation_temperature_conditions|thermal considerations]].^[8085135b0a96974f:High-Voltage Cable Systems]
