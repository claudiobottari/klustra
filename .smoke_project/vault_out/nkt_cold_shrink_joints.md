## Overview

NKT cold shrink joints are pre-moulded, cold-shrinkable accessories designed for the jointing of high-voltage power cables in the 66–170 kV range. They are manufactured by NKT (formerly NKT Cables) and are approved for use in high-voltage cable systems alongside other type-tested accessories from suppliers such as Prysmian.^[f55e45b580b53352:0]

## Construction and materials

NKT cold shrink joints are factory-assembled, pre-moulded units that are expanded over a removable support core. During installation, the core is withdrawn, allowing the joint body to shrink onto the cable core without the need for heat or specialised tooling. The joint body is typically made from a silicone rubber or EPDM rubber compound that provides high dielectric strength and long-term stability. The joint includes integral stress control elements (e.g., capacitive or resistive field grading) and is designed to match the geometry of the cable's [[semi_conductive_screen]], [[metallic_sheath]], and [[outer_protective_jacket]].

## Performance and testing

NKT cold shrink joints are type-tested in accordance with [[iec_62067]] (power cables with extruded insulation and their accessories for rated voltages above 150 kV up to 500 kV) and are also routinely tested for [[high_voltage_withstand_test]], [[partial_discharge_measurement]], and [[conductor_resistance_check]]. After installation, the joint is subjected to [[dc_vlf_withstand_testing]] and [[sheath_integrity_test]] to verify the quality of the assembly.^[f55e45b580b53352:0]

## Application range

The joints are approved for use on cables with [[cross_linked_polyethylene_insulation]] (XLPE) and are suitable for installation in [[underground_urban_networks]], [[submarine_crossings]], and [[offshore_wind_farms]]. They are designed to operate within the full temperature range of XLPE cables (up to 90 °C continuous, 130 °C emergency) and to meet the [[design_lifetime]] requirements of the cable system (typically 40 years).

## Installation requirements

Installation of NKT cold shrink joints must follow the manufacturer's instructions and the project's [[company_specific_safety_procedures]]. Key requirements include:
- [[minimum_bending_radius]] of the cable during jointing must not be exceeded.
- [[installation_temperature]] must be within the range specified by NKT (typically –20 °C to +50 °C).
- The joint bay must be prepared according to [[joint_bay_dimensions]] and must be dry and clean.
- [[cable_isolation_and_earthing]] and [[voltage_absence_verification]] must be performed before any work begins.
- After jointing, the joint is tested as part of the [[after_installation_testing]] programme.

## Approved supplier status

NKT cold shrink joints are listed on the approved supplier list for high-voltage cable accessories, alongside Prysmian pre-moulded joints and terminations. The approval is based on successful type testing to [[iec_62067]] and compliance with [[local_electrical_safety_regulations]].^[f55e45b580b53352:0]
