## Overview

Cable isolation and earthing are critical safety procedures performed before any work on high-voltage (HV) cable systems. These steps ensure that cables are de-energized, verified as safe, and protected against accidental re-energization. Compliance with international, local, and company-specific standards is mandatory.

## Regulatory Framework

All HV cable work must comply with:
- **IEC 61936-1** (Power installations exceeding 1 kV) ^[46d617e505500254:1]
- Local electrical safety regulations ^[46d617e505500254:1]
- Company-specific safety procedures ^[46d617e505500254:1]

## Safe Working Practices

Before any work on cable systems, the following steps must be performed in order:
1. **Isolate and earth the cable at both ends** – This ensures the cable is disconnected from all power sources and any stored charge is safely discharged. ^[46d617e505500254:2]
2. **Verify absence of voltage** using approved voltage indicators. ^[46d617e505500254:2]
3. **Apply safety locks and tags** to prevent inadvertent re-energization. ^[46d617e505500254:2]
4. **Establish a safe work zone with barriers** to protect personnel and the public. ^[46d617e505500254:2]

## Emergency Procedures

In case of a cable fault during live operation:
- Maintain an **exclusion zone of 3 m** around the suspected fault location. ^[46d617e505500254:3]
- **Do not attempt to re-energize** without inspection. ^[46d617e505500254:3]
- **Report immediately** to the control centre. ^[46d617e505500254:3]
