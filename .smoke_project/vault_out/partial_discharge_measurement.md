Partial discharge (PD) measurement is a non-destructive diagnostic technique used to detect and quantify localized electrical discharges that occur within the insulation of high-voltage equipment, such as [[xlpe_insulation]] cables. These discharges do not completely bridge the gap between conductors but can progressively degrade the insulation, leading to eventual failure.

## Application in Cable Testing

PD measurement is a mandatory routine test for every manufactured length of high-voltage cable. The test is performed to ensure the integrity of the insulation system, with a maximum acceptable discharge level of less than 5 pC (picocoulombs).^[362871cd3841da0e:1] This threshold is specified in standards such as [[iec_62067]].

In addition to routine testing, PD measurement is also part of the type testing regime for cable systems. Type tests, which validate the design of the cable system, include electrical tests such as withstand voltage, partial discharge, and tan delta measurements.^[362871cd3841da0e:1]

## Measurement Principles

Partial discharge measurement involves applying a high voltage to the cable and using sensitive detection equipment to capture the small electrical pulses generated by discharges within the insulation. The test is typically performed at a voltage level that stresses the insulation to a degree sufficient to reveal any defects, but without causing immediate breakdown.

## Importance for Cable Reliability

Regular PD measurement is essential for assessing the condition of [[high_voltage_cable_systems]], particularly those with [[xlpe_insulation]] which can be susceptible to degradation mechanisms such as water treeing and electrical treeing. By detecting PD activity early, maintenance can be planned to prevent in-service failures.

## Related Tests

PD measurement is often performed alongside other diagnostic tests, such as [[high_voltage_withstand_test]], [[sheath_integrity_test]], and [[joint_resistance_measurement]], to provide a comprehensive assessment of cable system health.
